Week 3 Focus — From Your Intake
Transition Game + Ground Ball Fundamentals
Team wants to move the ball fast and attack before the defense sets up — but struggles with ground balls (rated 3/5) and lacks answers when the other team adjusts. Same root cause: players are thinking about the ball, not reading the field. This week resolves it by building automatic scoop-to-outlet habits and teaching a simple 3v2 read structure that works under pressure.
End of Week Outcome: Players scoop ground balls cleanly and immediately look for an outlet pass. The transition unit (top 4) reads a 3v2 advantage without hesitation and finishes with a quality shot 7 out of 10 times.
Conditioning — Field Lacrosse Standard
Mon / Wed: 4 × 40y shuttle sprints · full recovery between reps · two groups.
Tue / Thu: 6 × 20y acceleration sprints · focus on first three steps · full recovery.
Fri: No conditioning load — activation only (partner passing warm-up).
Week 4: Add 2 reps to Mon/Wed shuttles.
Goalie Track — Parallel Focus (0–15 min)  ·  Strong returning goalie — continue building consistency
All days: Dynamic warm-up (high knees, butt kicks, carioca, backpedal) + partner passing (stick side, off-stick side, high, low).
Mon / Wed (Positioning + Angle Work): 4 × 5-min blocks — Arc positioning drills (coach moves ball around the arc, goalie mirrors), Step-to-the-shot drill (no saves, just footwork), Angle cuts (coach shoots from different spots, goalie works top-hand positioning).
Tue / Thu (Save Technique): 4 × 5-min blocks — High save reps (forehead holds, two hands on the stick), Low save reps (stick to the ground, hips back), Rapid-fire close-range (coach 5 yards out, soft feeds, volume over power).
Fri (Game Prep): Warm-up only. Joins team for all live scrimmage reps. Notes where every shot goes (high/low, stick-side/off-stick).

COACHING NOTES
This Week's Priorities
  • Monday: Establish clean ground-ball mechanics. Every player leaves Monday knowing "butt down, top hand down, scoop through it."
  • Tuesday: Teach the 3v2 read structure. Players must understand: spread wide, pass to the open man, attack when you have space.
  • Wednesday: Introduce settled offense concepts (pick-and-roll) without stalling the transition-first identity.
  • Thursday: Build defensive communication habits. Every possession should have a verbal "Ball, Help Left, Help Right" call.
  • Friday: Measure Week 3's two core outcomes: GB win rate and transition finish rate. Log every metric.
Evaluation Notes
  • Track which players consistently scoop cleanly under pressure — these are your primary GB battle starters.
  • Log outlet pass completion rate by player. If a player scoops well but doesn't look up, they need "eyes before you turn" cue.
  • Note which players finish 3v2 opportunities vs. which ones force bad shots. The finishers are your first-line transition unit.
  • Watch for defensive players who communicate out loud — reward them with callouts in Friday's cool-down.
  • Identify any player who consistently shoots off-balance or with feet moving. They need catch-and-shoot drills in Week 4.
  • Monitor midfield rotation fatigue. If players are gassed after 3 possessions, shorten shifts starting Thursday.
Watch For (U12-Specific Failure Modes)
  • Players bend at the waist instead of dropping the hips on ground balls — cue "butt down" every rep until it's automatic.
  • Ball-carrier scoops and immediately looks down at the ball instead of finding an outlet — this kills the transition game.
  • On 3v2 breaks, players bunch together instead of spreading wide — the advantage disappears if all three are within 5 yards.
  • Goalie clears too late — outlets should be available the moment the save is made, not 3 seconds later.
  • Defenders stop short of the ball-carrier on help defense and give a free shooting lane.
  • Players cradle with the top hand choked up on the plastic instead of down the shaft — this limits reach and control.
