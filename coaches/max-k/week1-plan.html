Week 1 Focus — From Your Intake
Establish Wing-Driven Counter Attack System — Primary 1v0 & 2v1 Scoring Opportunities
EA's counter attack is the main scoring source, but wing play is thin (2 developed players at positions 2/4). This week builds the foundation: wings learn to create separation on outlet, recognize numbers-up situations, and finish primary counter opportunities consistently. All defensive work feeds counter attack rehearsal — every transition creates a teaching moment.
End of Week Outcome
By Friday's scrimmage, wings recognize and convert 7/10 primary counter opportunities (1v0 or 2v1), and the starting GK (elite, returning All-League) consistently delivers the outlet pass to the correct wing within 2 seconds of possession.
Swim Conditioning — Week 1 Protocol  ·  All sets head-up · no walls · 1:1 work:rest · two groups
All sets: head-up · no walls · 1:1 work:rest · two groups (Group 2 leaves when Group 1 touches far end). Time every rep.
Mon / Thu: 20 × 25y | Tue: 10 × 50y · :60 rest | Wed: 5 × 75y · 90s rest | Fri: 8 × 25y · full recovery
Week 2: add 2 reps, tighten rest 5s. Friday never overloads.
GK Track — Parallel Focus (0–40 min)  ·  Elite returning GK — outlet speed + counter attack recognition priority
Every day warm-up: 200y (25 free / 25 backstroke / 25 eggbeater / 25 vertical backstroke) + 3 × 100y technical block (GP · Lateral · Glide · Quick Hands · Cherry Pickers · Gravity Drill).

Mon / Wed (Day 1 — Positional): 4 × 7-min blocks · 20s work / 10s rest — GP hold, GP + corner lunges, Near-side glide series, Cross-cage glide series.
Tue / Thu (Day 2 — Heavy Ball): 4 × 7-min blocks · heavy ball throughout — Forehead holds, Med ball two-handed lunges, Lateral lunges (heavy ball), 25 catapult throws.
Fri (Activation only): 100y warm-up + 1 × 100y technical set. Joins team for all special teams reps. Notes where every shot goes — high/low, near/far.

GK communication priorities: Call "set" on every 5x6 rep (GK owns the shape). Louder when shape breaks, not quieter. Verbalize counter attack read: "outlet left / outlet right / hold" on every save.

COACHING NOTES
Preseason Priorities — Week 1
  • Decision Monday: Identify top 3 wing players by name after the Counter Attack Lanes drill. These players mentor developing wings in paired reps Tuesday–Thursday.
  • Teaching sequence: Counter attack (Mon) → Defense (Tue) → Offense (Wed) → Counter finishing (Thu) → Integration (Fri). Build complexity daily but never add a new concept until the previous day's focal point is clean under light pressure.
  • Wing development urgency: Only 2 developed players at positions 2/4. Every session includes a "mentor pairing" — elite wing works alongside developing wing in at least one drill block. Log progress by name.
  • GK integration: Elite returning GK runs the counter attack outlet system. GK must verbalize "outlet left / outlet right / hold" on every save by Wednesday. This is the communication standard — louder when pressure increases.
  • Scrimmage focus: Track the six KPIs in every scrimmage block (Mon, Tue, Wed, Thu, Fri). Stop play only when the same failure mode appears 3 times in a row — address it immediately, then resume.
Scrimmage / Evaluation Notes
  • Counter attack conversion rate: Log every 1v0 and 2v1 attempt. Count as "converted" if the possession results in a shot on cage within 10 sec of the outlet pass. Target: ≥70% by Friday.
  • GK outlet timing: Time from save to outlet pass. Count outlets delivered within 2 sec as "on-time." Track by GK name (if multiple goalies rotate). Flag any outlet delayed beyond 3 sec for immediate review.
  • Wing separation quality: Log by player name. "Clean separation" = wing receives outlet without defender contact. If the same wing struggles 3+ times in a session, pair them with an elite wing in the next session's first drill.
  • Splitting defense integrity: Count box entries vs. perimeter shots. Accept perimeter shots; box entry is a failure. If more than 2 box entries in any scrimmage period, tighten gap spacing by 6 inches in the next defensive drill.
  • Center-forward entry success: Track possessions where the ball reaches center-forward. Target: ≥60%. If entry rate falls below 50%, adjust 6x5 rotation to create more 2v1 opportunities at posts before advancing perimeter passing.
  • Rotation depth: Rotate lines every 5 min in Thursday and Friday scrimmages. Log which players maintain quality when fatigued — this informs the first-game depth chart.
Watch For — Common Failure Modes
  • Wings breaking too early: Wing sprint-swims to outlet before GK secures possession → defender intercepts the outlet pass. Cue: "Wait for the GK to turn — outlet is a race, not a guess."
  • GK holding the ball too long: GK scans for the "perfect" outlet instead of delivering the first open look. If outlet is delayed beyond 2 sec more than twice in a session, review Counter Attack Lanes drill (Create phase) with live pressure.
  • Center defender chasing perimeter passes: Defender leaves the gap to pressure a perimeter pass → creates a box-entry lane for center-forward. Cue: "Stay in your gap — perimeter shots are acceptable."
  • Wings drifting to center on outlet: Wing receives outlet pass but drifts toward the middle of the pool instead of staying wide → eliminates the numbers-up advantage. Cue: "Wide lane — make them choose."
  • Counter attack finishing hesitation: Wing receives outlet cleanly but hesitates to shoot 1v0 → defender recovers and eliminates the advantage. Cue: "Shoot the 1v0 — every time. No thinking."
  • Front-court settling too fast: Team receives counter attack outlet but immediately slows to set up front-court offense → eliminates transition scoring opportunities. If this appears 3+ times, add a constraint in the next scrimmage: "Must shoot within 10 sec of crossing half or possession resets."
