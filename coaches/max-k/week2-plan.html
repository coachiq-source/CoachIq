COACHING NOTES
Game Week Priorities
  • Center defender must get ball-side BEFORE the pass to Jacob — if he catches with inside position, we're already beaten. Drill this sequence Mon/Wed until it's automatic.
  • Counter attack tempo is the adjustment they asked for — "slow down" means complete the outlet-wing-center sequence before any drive. Don't let players rush the first pass.
  • Help-side slides must include a verbal "help" call AND a sprint-swim recovery to the perimeter mark. If the helper doesn't recover, PC's shooters will punish us.
  • Perimeter shooting is the answer to their zone — they're giving us outside shots. Build confidence in Thu/Fri sessions so players shoot with conviction on game day.
  • GK communication is critical: "set" on every 5x6 rep, "outlet left/right" on every counter, "help left/right" to direct slides. The GK owns the defensive shape.
Scrimmage / Evaluation Notes
  • Track post-defense quality by player name — log every time the center defender holds position for 6+ seconds vs. when they get pushed off. If the same player struggles all week, consider rotating to a different defender.
  • Counter attack completion rate is the primary metric — count turnovers vs. finishes (shot or goal). If completion stays under 60%, slow the tempo even more in Week 3.
  • Watch for help-side recovery — are defenders sprinting back to their mark, or are they drifting? If perimeter shooters are open after slides, we haven't drilled the recovery hard enough.
  • GK outlet decisions — is the GK holding the ball waiting for a perfect outlet (bad), or delivering within 2 sec even under pressure (good)? Log outlet timing on every counter attempt.
  • Perimeter shooting percentage vs. their zone — are we taking the open shot, or forcing drives into traffic? If shooting % is low, it's a confidence issue, not a skill issue.
  • Jacob's touches at the post — how many times does he catch with inside position? Every catch where our defender is NOT ball-side is a teaching moment for the next practice.
Watch For
  • Center defender standing flat-footed at the post instead of keeping feet active — if the defender stops moving, Jacob will push them off. Cue: "Feet active, sprint-swim to recover position."
  • GK holding the ball too long on outlets — by the time the GK looks up, the wings are already covered. Cue: "Wing first within 2 seconds — if it's not there, swing middle."
  • Help-side wing sliding to the ball but NOT recovering to their perimeter mark — this leaves PC's shooters wide open. Cue: "Sprint-swim back before the ball gets there."
  • Counter attack lanes getting clogged because players are forcing drives before completing the outlet-wing-center sequence. Cue: "Three passes before any drive."
  • Perimeter shooters hesitating when they have a clean look — they're waiting for a "better" shot that won't come against a zone. Cue: "Shoot when you're open — PC is giving you this shot."
  • GK not verbalizing decisions on counter outlets — the wings don't know which side to break to. Cue: "Call outlet left or outlet right on every save."
