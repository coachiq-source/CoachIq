Week 1 Focus — From Your Intake
Speed Without Size — Maximize What You Have
Fast, skilled, small players with an elite goalie. Thin at 2-meter offense and center defender. Same root: lacking size and depth at critical positions. This week resolves it by building a transition-first identity — win with counter-attack speed before opponents can set their defense, and run a press base that denies easy entries rather than fighting size-on-size battles.

End of Week 1 Outcome: Your transition counter attack runs at game speed in 3v2 and 4v3 situations, converting 70%+ of numerical advantages. Your press defense forces perimeter passing errors and denies easy center-forward entries. Your goalie is the counter-attack trigger — every save turns into an outlet read within 2 seconds.

COACHING NOTES
Preseason Priorities
  • Install counter-attack identity first — it plays to your speed and goalie strength, and masks size disadvantage at center-forward and center defender.
  • Press defense is your base, but discipline beats aggression. Teach "stick in lane, don't lunge" on Day 1; over-aggressive press gets beaten by skip passes.
  • 6x5 power play: score off the exclusion call (quick first look) or reset to second look. Don't force low-percentage shots early in the clock.
  • GK is the counter-attack trigger. Every save → outlet read within 2 sec. If GK hesitates, the counter dies.
  • Thin at 2m offense and center D: by end of Week 1, name your starters at both positions and give them 70% of live reps. Depth comes later; consistency comes first.
Scrimmage / Evaluation Notes
  • Track counter-attack attempts vs conversions by day. Target: 70%+ by Friday. If below 60% on Thursday, slow down the transition — players are forcing shots instead of reading the defense.
  • Log center-forward entry passes attempted vs completed. If opponents complete 50%+ → tighten center D positioning, sink 1 meter deeper on the flat.
  • Time GK outlets (save to pass). Target ≤ 2 sec. If avg > 3 sec → reduce GK parallel track complexity in Week 2, add dedicated outlet drill.
  • Watch for press defenders lunging on ball fakes. Cue: "feet, not hands." Lunging breaks the press and opens skip-pass lanes.
  • 6x5: track first-look shots vs second-look shots. Target 60%+ first-look. If below → add "Primary Closed" drill to Week 2.
  • Note which players communicate on defense (call "ball," "help," "set"). Vocal leaders at small programs carry defensive identity; identify them early.
Watch For
  • Small players trying to fight size-on-size at center D. Cue: "position beats size — stay ball-side, use the press to deny the pass before it arrives."
  • Counter attack turning into a footrace with no spacing. Cue: "lanes, not chaos — left wing, center, right wing. GK reads the open lane."
  • GK hesitating on outlet because they don't trust the read. Cue: "outlet to any open lane within 2 sec. If nothing open, call 'hold' — don't force it."
  • Press defenders ball-watching instead of denying passing lanes. Cue: "see the ball, but your job is the pass, not the ball."
  • 6x5 stalling when first look is covered. Cue: "if it's not open in 3 sec, reset to point and run second look. No standing."
  • Swimmers walking back to position after a shot (fatigue drill → game habit). Cue: "sprint-swim to recover. Every time."
