Week 1 Focus — Derived From Your System
Speed without structure kills the counter attack. Press breaks that don't convert — and a 6x5 unit that fires before it's set — turn your biggest weapon into a turnover.
Your identity is pace — fastest complete team in conference. But counter-attack primary with no traditional center-forward means front-court attack stalls the moment the quick counter doesn't convert. This week installs the structure that makes speed a weapon: press discipline feeding clean counter attack lanes, 6x5 quick execution with defined recovery roles, and a first-year GK taking communication ownership of the 5x6 shape.
End of Week 1 Outcome
Every press break produces a sprint-swim into a named counter attack lane — no freelancing, no hesitation. The 6x5 unit scores or exits cleanly within 20 seconds. The GK calls every 5x6 shape break before any field player adjusts.
Swim Conditioning — Suggested Sets
All sets: head-up · no walls · 1:1 work:rest · two groups (Group 2 leaves when Group 1 touches far end). Time every rep.
Mon / Thu: 20 × 25y  |  Tue: 10 × 50y · :60 rest  |  Wed: 5 × 75y · 90s rest  |  Week 2: add 2 reps, tighten rest 5s.
GK Track — Parallel Focus (0–40 min)  ·  GK communication owns 5x6 shape and drives counter attack initiation.
All days: 200y warm-up (25 free / 25 backstroke / 25 eggbeater / 25 vertical backstroke) + 3 × 100y technical block (GP · Lateral · Glide · Quick Hands · Cherry Pickers · Gravity Drill).
Mon / Wed (Day 1 — Positional): 4 × 7-min blocks · 20s work / 10s rest → GP hold → GP + corner lunges → near-side glide series → cross-cage glide series.
Tue / Thu (Day 2 — Heavy Ball): 4 × 7-min blocks · heavy ball throughout → forehead holds → med ball two-handed lunges → lateral lunges → 25 catapult throws.
Fri (Activation only): 100y warm-up + 1 × 100y technical set. Joins team for all special teams reps. Notes where every shot goes — high or low, near-side or far-side.
Week 1 Tracking Priorities
Counter Attack Lane Discipline
Track by player name — who swims to the named lane vs. who freelances on every press break
If same players freelance 3+ times → assign fixed lane ownership in Week 2
Counter Attack Conversion Rate
Log every attempt: primary (2v1), transition (3v2/4v3), secondary
If conversion <40% → reduce to 3v2 only and drill the Advantage Rule verbalization
6x5 Quick — Time to Shot
Track whether the quick is attempted within 5 seconds of exclusion call. Log attempts vs. conversions
If quick attempt rate <60% → defenders not recovering immediately; drill exclusion drawer recovery
5x6 Shape Breaks
Count broken shapes per rep — who is out of position and when. GK calls each break
If GK calls are late → GK communication timing is the problem; drill in warm-up
Press Recovery — Time to Reset
Track how quickly press resets after a turnover or save. Target: full press re-established within 2 possessions
If reset is slow → press triggers are unclear; define the verbal reset cue in Week 2
GK Communication Ownership
Count unprompted GK shape calls during 5x6 and scrimmage. Target: GK calls before any field player adjusts
If field players are calling shape → GK is reactive; add GK comm reps to every warm-up in Week 2
Week 2 Adjustment Trigger
If 2 or more KPIs fall below target at the first scrimmage or game → repeat the Week 1 focal theme in Week 2 with reduced complexity. Add no new concepts until counter attack lane discipline, 6x5 quick execution, and GK 5x6 communication are consistent under live pressure.

SpideringMon / Wed / Thu / Fri warm-up Open water. Hollow upper body, lower core engaged. Scull forward, sideways, backward, 360-degree rotation both directions. 5–8 min each session. Stationary → moving. "Hollow the upper body — coiled, not rigid. Every defensive movement starts here." Players move in all directions without losing eggbeater rhythm by end of Week 1.
Grab-and-Go PreventionMon · 45–70 min 5v5 press. Beaten defender wins hand fight, disengages, sprint-swims to named counter attack lane. Offense counters immediately. 3 × 8-min periods. No stoppages — consequences are live. "Win the hand fight — then sprint-swim. You cannot recover a lane you are still tangled in." Lane discipline frequency improves from period 1 to period 3. Top 3 lane breakers identified by session end.
Advantage Rules — 3v2 / 4v3Tue · 20–50 min GK outlet initiates. Establish right, center, left lanes. Verbalize rule before executing. 15 reps at 3v2, 15 reps at 4v3. 20-second recovery swim between reps. "Say the rule before you swim — verbalization forces the read." ≥65% conversion 3v2; ≥50% 4v3. First-line counter attack group selected by session end.
6x5 Quick — Exclusion to ShotWed · 20–45 min 6x5, quick-first structure. Exclusion drawer recovers immediately. Shot or clean exit within 20 seconds. 10 clean reps before adding defensive pressure. Then 10 live reps with shot clock. "The exclusion drawer's recovery is the first action — not a spectator moment." ≥60% quick attempt rate within 5 seconds. Primary 6x5 unit locked by session end.
Transition Offense — No Center-ForwardThu · 20–45 min 5v5: transition into front-court when counter doesn't convert. Drive-and-replace + skip pass structure. 3 × 8-min periods. Track possessions requiring 3+ passes before shot attempt. "Drive creates the gap. The skip freezes the zone. Pick one — execute it." Possessions requiring 3+ passes drops from period 1 to period 3. No camping, constant motion.
Timeout ProtocolFri · 78–88 min Three scenarios: tied / down one / up one. Two adjustments, one positive, under 60 seconds, restart. 3 simulated timeouts. Vary score and clock each time. "Two adjustments, one positive, 60 seconds. Everything else is noise." Zero hesitation on restart by rep 3.
