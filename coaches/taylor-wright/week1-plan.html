Week 1 Focus — From Your Intake
The center defender position is the week's critical unknown. High skill returns — but the defensive spine does not.
A press with logical zone on the center-forward only works if the center defender can hold position under physicality. Haverford is the #1 priority opponent specifically because they are physical. This week finds out who can handle that before Sept 1. Simultaneously: the 4-2 unit installs the 2v1 post rotation so the 6x5 doesn't stall when the first look is contested. Two decisions, three practices.
End of Week 1 Outcome
Center defender starter is named before the first game — that player runs all hole D reps in Friday's scrimmage, not as a candidate but as the starter. The 4-2 6x5 unit executes the post rotation read without a verbal cue on 6 of 8 live reps. The drive-and-replace motion stays continuous under defensive pressure.
Swim Conditioning — Suggested Sets
All sets: head-up · no walls · 1:1 work:rest · two groups (Group 2 leaves when Group 1 touches far end). Time every rep.
Mon: 20 × 25y  |  Wed: 5 × 75y · 90s rest  |  Fri: 8 × 25y · full recovery  |  Week 2: add 2 reps, tighten rest 5s.
GK Track — Parallel Focus (0–40 min)  ·  GK communication drives logical zone shape and center-forward entry denial.
All days: 200y warm-up (25 free / 25 backstroke / 25 eggbeater / 25 vertical backstroke) + 3 × 100y technical block (GP · Lateral · Glide · Quick Hands · Cherry Pickers · Gravity Drill).
Mon / Fri (Day 1 — Positional): 4 × 7-min blocks · 20s work / 10s rest → GP hold → corner lunges → near-side glide → cross-cage glide.
Wed (Day 2 — Heavy Ball): 4 × 7-min blocks → forehead holds → med ball lunges → lateral lunges → 25 catapult throws.
All sessions: GK joins team for all special teams reps. Calls logical zone shape before any field player adjusts. Tracks shot location every rep.
Week 1 Tracking Priorities
Center Defender Evaluation
2 candidates — track entry pass read timing and contact decision under escalating physicality each session
Decision must be made before Friday's scrimmage — starter named, not candidate
6x5 Post Rotation Read
Track whether the 4-2 unit reads the 2v1 at the posts or reverts to perimeter stall when primary look is contested
If rotation read rate <60% → drill the read trigger in isolation before adding shot clock
Drive-and-Replace Continuity
Count possessions where motion stalls — ball stays one side 3+ passes without a gap drive attempt
If stall rate >40% → players are camping; reset the replace rule before next live rep
Logical Zone — Center-Forward Entry Denial
Count center-forward entry passes completed vs. denied. GK tracks each attempt and calls the shape adjustment
If >3 entries per scrimmage → center defender footwork is the problem, not the scheme
Transition Counter Rate
Log 3v2 and 4v3 attempts vs. conversions. This team's counter emphasis — baseline it Week 1
If conversion <45% → GK outlet timing; the counter starts at the save, not after it
Physicality Under Pressure
Track which players hold position under contact in the center defender drill. High skill ≠ physicality — log who competes
If no candidate holds position → escalate physicality each round until one emerges; evaluate vs. Haverford
Week 2 Adjustment Trigger
If center defender is still unresolved after Friday's scrimmage → make the decision anyway and give the named starter all reps Week 2. If 6x5 rotation read rate is below 60% → repeat the post rotation drill before adding shot clock pressure. Do not add new offensive concepts until the center defender is settled and the 4-2 rotation is clean.

Coaching Notes — Week 1
Preseason Priorities
  • Center defender starter named before Friday scrimmage — no later
  • 6x5 primary unit locked by end of Wednesday — draws, quick, post rotation call
  • High skill ≠ physicality — the evaluation must test contact, not just technique
  • New concepts before swim sets — teach before fatigue, not after
  • Ball / Player / Area — repeat until second nature on every press rep
    • Scrimmage Evaluation
  • Evaluation only — do not coach to the result in live reps
  • Log center-forward entry attempts vs. denials every scrimmage
  • Log 6x5 rotation reads: before or after shot clock pressure
  • Log transition counter attempts vs. conversions — Week 1 baseline
  • Compare drive-and-replace stall rate across all three sessions
  • GK tracks every shot location — high/low, near/far, every rep
    • Watch For
  • Center defender "skill-playing" physicality — needs to make contact, not avoid it
  • 6x5 unit waiting for the shot clock before reading the post rotation
  • Drive-and-replace players camping after the drive — replacement is the drill
  • GK going quiet on logical zone shape breaks — must call before any field player moves
  • Transition counter launching without the Advantage Rule verbalized
  • Haverford prep: their physicality in the hole will test whoever you name — the decision matters
