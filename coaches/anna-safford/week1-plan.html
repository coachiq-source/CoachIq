Week 1 Focus — From Your Intake
Defenders overcommitting and killing the primary counter attack. Front-court attack stalling when the first 6x5 look is taken away.
Same problem: players reacting to what is in front of them instead of reading the full picture. Speed is your identity — this week gives it structure. Press base position without weight commitment. 6x5 second look as a trained read. Counter attack lanes defined before they are needed.
End of Week 1 Outcome
Defenders recover the counter attack lane immediately on every press break — no passive swim, no hesitation. The 6x5 unit shifts to a second look before the stall, and the primary counter attack group executes the Advantage Rule without a verbal prompt.
Swim Conditioning — Suggested Sets
All sets: head-up · no walls · 1:1 work:rest · two groups (Group 2 leaves when Group 1 touches far end). Time every rep.
Mon / Thu: 20 × 25y  |  Tue: 10 × 50y · :60 rest  |  Wed: 5 × 75y · 90s rest  |  Fri: 8 × 25y · full recovery  |  Week 2: add 2 reps, tighten rest 5s.
GK Track — Parallel Focus (0–40 min)  ·  GK communication drives 5x6 shape and counter attack initiation.
All days: 200y warm-up (25 free / 25 backstroke / 25 eggbeater / 25 vertical backstroke) + 3 × 100y technical block (GP · Lateral · Glide · Quick Hands · Cherry Pickers · Gravity Drill).
Mon / Wed (Day 1 — Positional): 4 × 7-min blocks · 20s work / 10s rest · GP hold → GP + corner lunges → near-side glide series → cross-cage glide series.
Tue / Thu (Day 2 — Heavy Ball): 4 × 7-min blocks · heavy ball throughout · forehead holds → med ball two-handed lunges → lateral lunges → 25 catapult throws.
Fri (Activation only): 100y warm-up + 1 × 100y technical set. Joins team for all special teams reps. Notes where every shot goes — high or low, near-side or far-side.
Week 1 Tracking Priorities
Overcommit Instances
Track by player name — Mon, Thu, and Wilson scrimmage
If same players appear all three days → reduce press aggression in Week 2 scrimmage block
Counter Attack — Attempts vs. Conversions
Log every attempt: primary, transition, or secondary
If conversion <40% → simplify lane spacing next session; check Advantage Rule verbalization
6x5 — First Look vs. Second Look
Track which look produces the shot and whether it was a stall or a read
If second-look rate <50% on "closed" reps → drill the read earlier in Week 2
5x6 Shape Breaks
Count broken shapes per rep — who is out of position and when
If breaks persist → GK calls are late; work GK communication timing in warmup
Center Defender Evaluation
2 candidates — track entry pass reads and contact decision timing
Decision must be made by end of Thursday — starter named before Sept 11
Timeout Restart Clarity
Players re-set in under 5 seconds on the restart signal
If hesitation persists at rep 3 → run one more rep at Wilson before first real timeout
Week 2 Adjustment Trigger
If 2 or more KPIs fall below target at Wilson → repeat the Week 1 focal theme in Week 2 with reduced complexity. Add no new concepts until press recovery, counter attack lane discipline, and 6x5 second-look reads are consistent under live pressure.

DrillSetupReps / DurationCoaching CueSuccess Metric
SpideringMon / Wed / Thu / Fri warm-up Open water. Hollow upper body, lower core engaged. Scull forward, sideways, backward, 360-degree rotation both directions. 5–8 min each session. Stationary → moving. "Hollow the upper body — coiled, not rigid. Every defensive movement starts here." Players move in all directions without losing eggbeater rhythm by end of Week 1.
Grab-and-Go PreventionMon · 45–75 min 5v5 press. Beaten defender wins hand fight, disengages, sprint-swims to primary counter attack lane. Offense counters immediately. 3 × 8-min periods. No stoppages — consequences are live. "Win the hand fight — then sprint-swim. You cannot recover a lane you are still tangled in." Overcommit frequency drops from period 1 to period 3. Top 3 offenders identified by session end.
Advantage Rules — 3v2 / 4v3Tue · 20–50 min GK outlet initiates. Establish right, center, left lanes. Verbalize rule before executing. 15 reps at 3v2, 15 reps at 4v3. 20-second recovery swim between reps. "Say the rule before you swim — verbalization forces the read." ≥65% conversion 3v2; ≥50% 4v3. First-line counter attack group selected by session end.
"Primary Closed" — 6x5 Second LookWed · 50–80 min 6x5 in 3-3. Coach signals "closed" mid-possession — immediate shift to second look, no reset. 12 reps, "closed" on 8. Track: shot attempts vs. stalls vs. turnovers. "The read is due before the signal." ≥50% of "closed" reps produce a shot attempt. Primary 6x5 unit locked by session end.
Center Defender Ball-Side PositioningThu · 75–95 min Two candidates vs. live center-forward entries. Armpit grab points, breaststroke lunges, fronting with whistle-called changes. Escalate physicality each round. 3 rounds per candidate, escalating intensity. "Track who reads the entry pass earlier — that is your starter." Starter named before end of Thursday practice. No exceptions.
Timeout ProtocolFri · 90–105 min Three scenarios: tied / down one / up one. Two adjustments, one positive, under 60 seconds, restart. 3 simulated timeouts. Vary score and clock each time. "Two adjustments, one positive, 60 seconds. Everything else is noise." Zero hesitation on restart by rep 3. If not → one more rep at Wilson before first game timeout.
