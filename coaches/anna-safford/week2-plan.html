Week 1 Focus — From Your Intake
Players defaulting to set offense when they don't recognize the counter attack opportunity. Front-court attack stalling when the first look is taken away.
Same problem: players reacting to what's in front of them instead of reading the full picture before deciding. This week builds recognition before movement — teaching the visual reads that separate primary counter attack from front-court setup, and the secondary options that keep the offense moving when the first look disappears.
End of Week Outcome
Players recognize counter attack lanes and attack them without hesitation. When the first look in front-court is denied, they immediately read second and third options instead of stalling. Team can sustain offensive motion for 25+ seconds without forced shots.

Coaching Notes
Preseason Priorities
  • Counter attack recognition must be taught before movement — show the visual reads (outlet open / outlet contested / no outlet) before running live reps
  • Front-court motion continuity depends on reading second options — players must learn to see the full picture, not just the first look
  • Alexandra McCarley (#9) is elite but too quiet — assign her verbal cue responsibilities by name (call "outlet left/right" on every counter attack)
  • Lilly Smolenski (#11) yells when she's frustrated — redirect her communication toward specific reads ("second look open" instead of "come on!")
  • This team is in the mixed phase — experienced core (seniors) runs full-version drills, developing players (juniors/sophomores) start at entry level and progress when clean
Scrimmage / Evaluation Notes
  • Track counter attack recognition rate (outlet vs. setup) — log by player name, not just team total
  • Count front-court stalls (possessions that collapse without a quality look) — goal is < 5 per scrimmage by Friday
  • Log 6x5 shots by option (first look vs. second look) — if second look never appears, Week 2 forces it
  • Watch Grace Christie (#1, GK) communication on 5x6 — does the team respond to "set" or ignore it?
  • Evaluate Siena Scungio (#2) shot selection — does she brick multiple shots in a row and shut down?
  • Note when Stellarose Ferst (freshman) arrives — expect her to be a major contributor, assess where she fits in counter attack lanes and 6x5 rotation
Watch For
  • Counter attack lanes collapsing into center — players must hold width until the pass arrives
  • Front-court motion stopping when the first look is denied — this is the root problem, watch for it in every 6v6 rep
  • Players walking in the water instead of sprint-swimming or recovery swimming — verify movement language every session
  • 6x5 forcing the first look when it's not there — second-look read must become automatic by end of week
  • 5x6 shape breaking when GK calls "set" — if this happens > 3 times per session, add shape drill to Week 2 warm-up
  • Team getting snippy after giving up 2+ goals in a row — Coach timeout to reset mentally, don't let it spiral
