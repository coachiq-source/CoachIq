Week 1 Focus — From Your Intake
Defensive identity is locked. The offense has no answer when a split defense takes away the drive — and no answer in the final 2 minutes when a lead has to be protected.
Same problem: the team knows how to create pressure but not how to solve it. This week installs a defined zone attack sequence — widen the triangle at 3 & 4, create the backdoor at 2 — and builds the 6x5 secondary read so the hybrid unit has a move when the 4-2 primary is denied. Press identity stays. Offensive structure gets added to it.
End of Week 1 Outcome
Players execute the triangle-stress zone attack sequence without a verbal prompt — widen, stress, read the backdoor. The 6x5 hybrid unit shifts to the secondary read before the shot clock forces a stall. In the final 2 minutes, the team executes one defined possession structure rather than improvising.
Swim Conditioning — Suggested Sets
All sets: head-up · no walls · 1:1 work:rest · two groups (Group 2 leaves when Group 1 touches far end). Time every rep.
Mon / Thu: 16 × 25y  |  Tue: 8 × 50y · :60 rest  |  Wed: 4 × 75y · 90s rest  |  Week 2: add 2 reps, tighten rest 5s. Note: 75 min sessions — swim sets follow new concept introduction, not after fatigue.
GK Track — Parallel Focus (0–30 min)  ·  GK shot-read development and 5x6 communication ownership — first-year starter priority.
All days: 150y warm-up (25 free / 25 backstroke / 25 eggbeater / 25 vertical backstroke / 25 lateral) + 2 × 100y technical block (GP · Lateral · Glide · Quick Hands).
Mon / Wed (Day 1 — Positional): 3 × 7-min blocks · 20s work / 10s rest → GP hold → corner lunges → near-side glide → cross-cage glide.
Tue / Thu (Day 2 — Heavy Ball): 3 × 7-min blocks → forehead holds → med ball lunges → 20 catapult throws.
GK in all scrimmage reps. Track shot location every rep — high/low, near/far. Call 5x6 shape before any field player moves.
Week 1 Tracking Priorities
Zone Attack Execution
Track whether players widen to 3 & 4 before attempting the backdoor — sequence discipline, not outcome
If sequence breaks → reset to dry walkthrough; don't run live reps until the read order is clean
6x5 Secondary Read Rate
Track whether the hybrid unit shifts to 3-3 before the stall — not after. Target: read before clock pressure.
If secondary read rate <50% → drill the shift trigger in isolation before adding defense
Final 2 Min Possession Structure
In scrimmage: when up one in final 2 min, does the offense run the defined structure or improvise?
If improvising → timeout protocol drill before first competitive game
Counter Attack Conversion
Log every primary counter attempt (1v0/2v1). This is the team's primary scoring source — baseline it now.
If conversion <50% → check GK outlet timing; the counter starts at the save
Center Defender Containment
Track how many times the opponent's center-forward scores or draws an exclusion against your hole D
If 3+ per scrimmage → explore double coverage trigger from position 3
GK 5x6 Shape Calls
Count unprompted GK shape calls. Target: GK calls before any field player adjusts — every rep
If GK is reactive → add verbal shape call to every warm-up set; ownership builds through repetition
Week 2 Adjustment Trigger
If 2 or more KPIs fall below target → repeat the zone attack sequence and 6x5 secondary read in Week 2 with reduced complexity. Do not add the late-game possession structure until the zone attack sequence and 6x5 shift are executing without prompts under live pressure.

6x5 Hybrid — 4-2 Primary + 3-3 Shift Read

Coaching Notes — Week 1
Preseason Priorities
  • Zone attack sequence (widen → stress → read) defined and clean before Easterns — this is the Week 1 non-negotiable
  • 6x5 primary unit locked by end of Tuesday — who draws, who takes the quick, who calls the shift
  • Double coverage trigger named by end of Thursday — not "sometimes" but "when X, we do Y"
  • Late-game possession structure defined before first competitive game
  • New concepts before swim sets — teach before fatigue, not after
    • Scrimmage Evaluation
  • Evaluation only in scrimmage reps — do not coach to the result
  • Log zone attack sequence use: how many possessions attempt it vs. revert to improvised perimeter play
  • Log 6x5 shift timing: before clock pressure or after
  • Log counter attack attempts vs. conversions — Week 1 primary scoring baseline
  • Note late-game scenarios: does the team execute structure or make individual decisions
  • GK notes where every shot goes — high/low, near/far — every rep
    • Watch For
  • Position 2 shooting before the triangle is widened — sequence must happen first
  • 6x5 unit waiting for the shot clock before shifting — the read triggers the shift, not the clock
  • Counter attack players freelancing instead of hitting the named lane
  • GK going quiet on 5x6 shape breaks — she must get louder, not quieter
  • Late-game improvising — any individual drive in the final 2 minutes that isn't the defined structure
  • Wing position (2/4) depth gap showing under press recovery — with 10 players, substitution timing matters
