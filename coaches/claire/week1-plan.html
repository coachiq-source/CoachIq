Week 1 Focus — From Your Intake
Players uncertain on basic positioning and ground-ball approach. Zone defense looks solid on paper but breaks when ball movement speeds up.
Same problem: players don't yet know where their body goes relative to the ball. This week resolves it by teaching one core principle — "ball, help left, help right" — and drilling the scoop-through-it ground-ball technique until it's automatic. Every session reinforces body positioning relative to the ball on both sides of the field.
End of Week 1 Outcome: Every player can state "ball, help left, help right" and demonstrate it in a live 3v3. Ground balls are scooped cleanly 7 out of 10 reps. Players understand zone-defense positioning well enough to hold a basic 2-2-2 shape for three consecutive passes.
Conditioning — Age-Appropriate Activation
Every session begins with a dynamic warm-up (8 min): high knees, butt kicks, lateral shuffle, carioca, walking lunges with a twist, arm circles, leg swings. Partner passing starts at 10 yards and increases to 15 yards. Session 1: 2 × 20-yard shuttle runs (light jog recovery). Session 2: 3 × 30-yard shuttle runs. All conditioning stays under 3 minutes total — U16 focus is skill development, not endurance.
Goalie Track — Position Unsettled (Coach Feeds Until Decided)
Goalie position is unsettled. While the coach decides, every shooting drill uses coach feeds to an empty net or a designated field player standing in as a placeholder goalie for live pressure only. Focus on shooter technique and accuracy, not goalie performance. Once a goalie is selected, begin a structured goalie warm-up: 200 soft catches (easy feeds, two hands, no sticks up) + 3 rounds of low / hip / high feeds. Communication priority: call "clear" when an outlet is available during ground-ball battles and clearing.
WEEK 1 TRACKING PRIORITIES
Ground Ball Win Rate
Scooped cleanly / contested GBs
If below 60% by Session 2 → add one extra 1v1 GB round every session and slow the drill tempo
Zone Shape Breaks
Count shifts where a player is >10 yards out of position
If more than 5 breaks per session → reduce live-play tempo and walk through the shape with a ball on a string
Passing Completion Rate
Catches / total passes in partner drills
If below 75% → shorten distance to 8 yards and emphasize "catch two hands" every rep
Ball-Help-Help Verbal Cues
Count of players who say it out loud during live play
If fewer than 10 players consistently say it → script it into every rep ("say it before you move")
Clear Success Rate
Clears that cross midfield / attempted clears
If below 70% → simplify to a 3-pass max clear and walk it through at half speed
Substitution Execution
Log sub errors (wrong line, missed call, timing issues)
If more than 2 errors per session → script subs by jersey color and name lines on a card
Week 2 Adjustment Trigger
If 2 or more KPIs fall below target at the first scrimmage → repeat the Week 1 focal theme in Week 2 with reduced complexity. Add no new concepts until ground-ball scooping, zone-shift communication, and basic passing under light pressure are consistent.

SESSION BREAKDOWN
Session 1
Ground Balls + Zone Intro
Priority: Every player leaves knowing "butt down, top hand down, scoop through it" and can demonstrate it once without correction.
0–8
Dynamic Warm-Up + Partner Passing
High knees, butt kicks, lateral shuffle, carioca, walking lunges with a twist, arm circles, leg swings. Partner passing at 10 yards, two hands, step to the target. Emphasize "catch two hands, look at your partner, then pass."
Activation
8–18
Noodle Scooping
Two lines, coach rolls a ball out, player scoops through with feet moving, fish-hook pivot away from imaginary pressure. Cue: "Butt down, top hand down, scoop through it." 8 reps per player.
Focal Drill Noodle Scooping
18–35
1v1 Ground Ball Battle
1v1 box drill, loose ball between two players. Winner scoops and completes an outlet pass to the coach. Cue: "Box out with your hips, then scoop away from pressure." 6 reps per player.
Focal Drill 1v1 GB's
35–50
Shell Drill — 2-2-2 Zone Shape
Walk through a 2-2-2 zone shape. Coach moves the ball slowly around the perimeter, defenders shift and call "ball, help left, help right" on every pass. No sticks up, no pressure — just positioning. 10 min of walk-through reps.
Zone Install
50–60
3v3 Half-Field
3v3 to a single goal. Offense passes around the arc, defense holds zone shape and calls "ball, help left, help right." Constraint: Offense must complete 3 passes before shooting. No checking — body positioning only. Coach feeds ground balls randomly; emphasize clean scoop technique.
Live Play
Decision Identify your top 3 ground-ball players by name — they start every GB battle in Session 2.
Session 2
Clearing + Set Offense Intro
Priority: Every clear crosses midfield with control. Players can name the three-pass clearing pattern (goalie → crease defender → wing) without prompting.
0–8
Dynamic Warm-Up + Partner Passing
Same warm-up as Session 1. Partner passing at 12 yards, emphasizing catch-look-pass rhythm. Add one round of "Bad Pass Drill" — partner throws a slightly off-target pass, receiver adjusts and catches two hands.
Activation
8–18
4v3 Box Clear
Goalie + 3 defenders, no ride. Goalie outlets to a wing, wing swings middle, carry over midline. Cue: "Goalie looks wing first; defenders spread wide." 8 reps, rotate groups.
Focal Drill 4v3 Box
18–35
4 Corners Passing + Star Drill
3v0 passing around the perimeter (top-wing-wing), catch-look-pass. Cue: "Catch two hands, look at the goal, then pass." Goal: 10 consecutive completions without a drop. Then add Star Drill: 5 players in a star, skip passes across the star.
Stick Skills 4 Corners Pass
35–50
2-Man Game (Pick & Roll)
2v2 on the wing, pick-and-roll live. Cue: "Set the pick with feet set, roll to the ball." Demonstrate once, then 5 min of live reps. Rotate pairs every 2 min.
Focal Drill 2-Man Game (Pick & Roll)
50–60
6v6 Scrimmage with Constraint
Full 6v6 to both goals. Constraint: Every possession must include at least one clear (start from the goalie) and one pick (offense sets a screen before shooting). Defense plays 2-2-2 zone, calls "ball, help left, help right" on every pass. Coach feeds 3 ground balls randomly.
Live Play
Decision Name the first-line clearing unit (4 players: goalie + 3 defenders) — they run every clear drill and the first two clears in every scrimmage.
FOCAL DRILLS — FULL DETAIL
Drill Setup Reps/Duration Coaching Cue What This Teaches Progression
Noodle Scooping Two lines, coach rolls a ball out, player scoops through, fish-hook pivot out. 8 reps per player "Butt down, top hand down, scoop through it." Ground balls are the foundation of possession — every game is won or lost on the ratio of GBs scooped cleanly. Entry: No pressure, slow roll, coach feedback on every rep. Full: Add a defender at half speed; winner must complete outlet.
1v1 GB's 1v1 box drill, loose ball between two players. Winner scoops and outlets to coach. 6 reps per player "Box out with your hips, then scoop away from pressure." Ground balls under pressure require body positioning first, stick positioning second. Entry: Defender starts 2 steps behind; attacker has advantage. Full: Even start, full speed, live outlet pass to a moving receiver.
4v3 Box Clear Goalie + 3 defenders, no ride. Outlet to wing, swing middle, carry over midline. 8 reps, rotate groups "Goalie looks wing first; defenders spread wide." Clearing is how a stopped shot or saved shot becomes offense — every turnover prevented in the defensive half is worth more than a made shot. Entry: Walk-through, no defense, goalie calls the outlet target. Full: Add 3 riders at half speed; clear must cross midline in under 10 sec.
4 Corners Pass / Star 3v0 passing around the perimeter (top-wing-wing), catch-look-pass. Then 5-player star with skip passes. 10 consecutive completions (4 Corners), then 5 min (Star) "Catch two hands, look at the goal, then pass." Settled offense is what happens when no one has a numbers advantage — the offense has to create an advantage by moving the ball and making the defense shift. Entry: 4 Corners only, 8-yard spacing, chest passes. Full: Star Drill with skip passes, 12-yard spacing, catch on the run.
2-Man Game (Pick & Roll) 2v2 on the wing, pick-and-roll live. Picker sets with feet, rolls to ball. 5 min, rotate pairs every 2 min "Set the pick with feet set, roll to the ball." Two-player combinations create scoring opportunities when the defense over-commits to one player. Entry: Walk-through, no defense — show the shape. Full: Live 2v2, defense can switch or stay; shooter must read and react.
Shell Drill (2-2-2 Zone) Defenders shift with the ball, no stick checks. Coach moves ball slowly around perimeter. 10 min walk-through "Ball, help left, help right." Zone defense requires every player to know where their body goes relative to the ball — not relative to an opponent. Entry: Walk-through, coach moves ball with hand, defenders shift in slow motion. Full: Live tempo, offense can pass freely, defense shifts and calls the rotation out loud.
COACHING NOTES
This Week's Priorities
  • Teach ground-ball scooping technique first — "butt down, top hand down, scoop through it" — before any contested drills.
  • Introduce zone defense as a shape-shift concept, not a matchup concept. Use "ball, help left, help right" as the verbal anchor.
  • Keep clearing simple: three passes max (goalie → crease defender → wing). No skip passes yet.
  • Every live-play block includes at least one ground ball. Make the scoop automatic before Week 2.
  • Substitutions: script them by jersey color and write down the lines. Call subs by name, not by waving.
Evaluation Notes
  • Watch for players who bend at the waist on ground balls instead of dropping the hips — cue "butt down" every rep.
  • Log which players consistently call "ball, help left, help right" during live play. These are your vocal leaders.
  • Track passing completion rate in partner drills. If below 75%, shorten the distance and slow the tempo.
  • Note any player who stops moving their feet during a catch or a scoop — footwork is the foundation.
  • Identify the top 3 ground-ball winners by name. They become your GB starters in every drill.
  • Watch for zone-shape breaks: any defender more than 10 yards out of position when the ball moves. If it happens more than 5 times in a session, walk the shape through again at half speed.
Watch For (Beginner Failure Modes)
  • Players bend at the waist instead of dropping the hips on ground balls.
  • Ball-carrier carries with the top hand on the plastic (choked up) — cue "hand down the shaft" every rep.
  • On zone shifts, a defender chases the ball instead of staying in their area — this is the most common break.
  • Players look at the ground when catching a pass instead of tracking the ball into their stick.
  • Shooter locks onto the corner and telegraphs the shot — teach a fake-at-center-shoot-corner rhythm.
  • Goalie position uncertainty leads to hesitation on clears — designate one player as the placeholder goalie for every drill until the position is settled.
