Week 1 Focus
Drive-and-replace motion offense requires players to read gaps, attack on time, and trust continuous movement. Splitting defense requires players to read box entry and accept perimeter shots.
End of Week: Players demonstrate gap recognition in drive-and-replace (3-on-3 live). Defenders identify box entry triggers and communicate defensive shape adjustments. Team executes 3-3 power play with first-look recognition. GK consistently calls defensive shape and outlet direction during counter attack transitions.

Drill Day Key Cue
Drive-and-Replace (3-on-3) Mon Verbalize gap before driving. Replace immediately when gap closes.
Splitting Defense (Pressure Passing) Tue Call shape before movement. Accept perimeter shots — protect the box.
3-3 Primary Look Wed Timing and spacing. Wing receives and drives immediately.
Primary Closed (6x5 second-look) Wed When first look closes, rotate immediately. Second look must open within 5 seconds.
5x6 Post Protection + GK Call Thu GK owns the shape. Defenders don't move until GK calls "set".
Counter Attack Lanes Mon/Thu/Fri GK calls outlet direction. Establish lanes before attacking. Lane discipline = conversion.

Watch For
  • Drive-and-replace: Players driving before gap opens
  • Splitting D: Defenders chasing ball vs. reading box entry
  • 6x5: Offense forcing first look when defended
  • 5x6: Defenders collapsing on perimeter
  • Counter: GK not calling outlet direction
  • Movement: Players walking (not sprint-swimming)
