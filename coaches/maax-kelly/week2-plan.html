Week 1 Focus — Preseason Foundation
Drive-and-Replace Motion with Gap Defense Principles
With only four days until the first game and limited decision-point data from the intake, this week focuses on installing the foundational framework: continuous motion offense tied to splitting/M-zone defense principles. The team has solid fundamentals and is ready for scheme installation. Success = players understand when to drive (vs. zone stress points) and how to replace behind the drive, while the defense learns to take away box entry and force perimeter.
End of Week Outcome
By Friday's scrimmage: Players execute drive-and-replace motion with correct spacing (3 & 4 wide vs. zone), defense forces 3+ perimeter passes before box entry in 5x6, and counter attack shows clear Create/Read/Attack phases in transition situations.

Coaching Notes
Preseason Priorities
  • Install offensive and defensive frameworks before Week 2 — this is scheme-building time
  • Teach drive-and-replace motion as a system, not individual skill — spacing and timing together
  • GK communication is a teaching priority — "set" calls must become automatic by first league game
  • Counter attack phases (Create / Read / Attack) are the bridge between defense and offense — emphasize GK outlet quality
  • Log every KPI daily — Week 2 plan depends on accurate data from this week
Scrimmage / Evaluation Notes
  • Track drive timing by player name — who creates open looks vs. who drives into congestion
  • Log replace behavior — standing and watching is a technical failure, not effort
  • Count box entries allowed vs. forced perimeter passes — defense success metric
  • Watch GK verbal communication volume — quieter when shape breaks = needs correction
  • Note counter attack breakdowns by phase (Create / Read / Attack) — tells you where to teach
  • First game is Sept 5 (4 days) — this week must produce game-ready baseline execution
Watch For
  • Players driving without reading zone stress points — creates congestion and turnovers
  • Standing after the drive instead of replacing behind — motion dies immediately
  • Center defender ball-watching instead of maintaining ball-side positioning — clean box entries result
  • GK silent on 5x6 reps — shape breaks without verbal ownership from the cage
  • Counter attack Create phase failure — GK outlet quality and fast swim to recover must be clean
  • 6x5 forcing first look when closed — teach pull-out and reset discipline before bad habits form
