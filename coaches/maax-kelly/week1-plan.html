Week 1 Focus — Installing Scheme Fundamentals
Drive-and-replace motion offense requires players to read gaps, attack on time, and trust continuous movement. Splitting defense requires players to read box entry and accept perimeter shots.
This team has solid fundamental skills but is installing scheme concepts for the first time. Week 1 establishes the baseline behaviors — reading gaps in offense, recognizing box entry patterns in defense — that all future tactics build from. Every drill starts at entry level and progresses only when execution is clean.
End of Week Outcome
Players demonstrate gap recognition in drive-and-replace (3-on-3 live). Defenders identify box entry triggers and communicate defensive shape adjustments. Team executes 3-3 power play with first-look recognition. GK consistently calls defensive shape and outlet direction during counter attack transitions.

COACHING NOTES
Preseason Priorities
  • This team is installing scheme for the first time — every drill starts at entry level before progressing. Do not rush to full versions until execution is clean.
  • Teach gap recognition (offense) and box entry reads (defense) before swim sets, not after fatigue. These are decision-making skills, not conditioning drills.
  • GK communication is foundational — "set" calls on 5x6 and outlet direction on counter attack must be consistent by Friday.
  • First game is Sept 5 (4 days after Week 1 starts) — this week establishes baseline behaviors that the team will refine all season.
  • Track every KPI by player name. Identify the 3–4 players who demonstrate consistent reads — these players anchor scheme installation in Week 2.
Scrimmage / Evaluation Notes
  • Track gap recognition instances by player name during drive-and-replace scrimmage (Mon, Fri). Note who verbalizes gaps and who reacts without reading.
  • Log box entry reads during splitting defense scrimmage (Tue, Fri). Count shape breaks — if more than 3 per session, simplify reads in Week 2.
  • Track counter attack conversion rate (Mon, Thu, Fri). Log outlet clarity (GK calls direction) and lane discipline (attackers establish before attacking).
  • Monitor 6x5 first-look vs. second-look success (Wed, Fri). If second look never opens, adjust rotation timing before Week 2.
  • Count 5x6 post protection breaks (Thu, Fri). GK must call "set" on every rep — if GK is silent, stop play and reset.
  • Watch for players who guess instead of read — these players need additional entry-level reps before progressing to full versions.
Watch For
  • Drive-and-replace: Players driving before gap opens or failing to replace when gap closes. Both are reading failures, not effort failures.
  • Splitting defense: Defenders chasing ball instead of reading box entry trigger. If shape breaks, stop play and replay with correct read.
  • 6x5: Offense forcing first look when it's defended. Teach "when first look closes, rotate immediately" — no hesitation.
  • 5x6: Defenders collapsing on perimeter instead of protecting posts. Reinforce "concede perimeter, protect the box" before every rep.
  • Counter attack: GK not calling outlet direction or attackers bunching lanes. Both kill conversion rate — address immediately.
  • General: Players walking (not sprint-swimming) during transitions. Every movement reference must be sprint-swim or recovery swim — no exceptions.
