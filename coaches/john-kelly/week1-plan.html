Week 1 Focus — From Your Intake
Building Center-Forward Entry Under Pressure
You're running a center-forward-dominant offense with only two seniors who can play, a thin 2-meter offense, and a roster that's building fundamentals. The gap is clear: your entire front-court attack depends on clean entry passes to post, but your GK is a liability (can't start the counter) and your press defense forces you to win possessions in transition. Same problem: your offense stalls when entry passes don't arrive, and your defense can't afford long possessions. This week resolves it by teaching your wings and drivers to create passing windows under press coverage, establishing a counter-attack rhythm that doesn't depend on GK reads, and drilling your center-forward to seal position and hold the ball when the entry arrives under pressure.

End of Week 1 Outcome: Your center-forward receives the ball cleanly in 7 out of 10 possessions by Friday's scrimmage. Wings create entry windows by pulling their defender high, then cutting backdoor. Drivers recognize when to skip the post and attack directly. Counter attack produces 3+ goals per scrimmage without depending on GK outlet reads.

COACHING NOTES
Preseason Priorities
  • Monday decision: Identify your 3 best entry-pass creators. They anchor every offensive possession this week.
  • Teaching sequence: Wing cuts → Post seal → Counter outlet. In that order. Don't teach counter reads until post positioning is clean.
  • GK development path: Your GK is a liability right now. Accept it. Build a two-touch counter protocol (GK to wing to driver) that removes the long-ball dependency. GK's job this week: accurate 5m outlets only.
  • Roster reality: You have 2 seniors who can play and 16 players building fundamentals. Every drill starts at the entry level. Do not progress to full versions until the entry version is clean 8/10 reps.
  • Friday outcome bar: 7/10 clean entry passes + 3+ counter goals + press holds outside shots. If you hit all three, Week 2 adds secondary counter reads and 6x5 second-look options. If you miss two, repeat Week 1 with no new concepts.
Scrimmage / Evaluation Notes
  • Entry-pass windows: Watch which wings create clean passing lanes. Log by name. The wings who pull-and-cut consistently are your starters.
  • Post seal quality: Does your center-forward's butt stay in the defender's hips when the pass arrives? If not, the entry pass is wasted.
  • Counter conversion rate: Track every 2v1, 3v2, 4v3. If under 60% conversion, your players are slowing down instead of attacking. Reteach: sprint first, finish fast.
  • Press overcommits: Count how many times a perimeter defender leaves their mark to chase the ball. Same 2 players? Pull them aside Thursday.
  • GK outlet accuracy: Is your GK hitting the called outlet, or guessing? If guessing, add 5 min of GK-to-wing outlet reps before every session next week.
  • Special teams execution: Your 4-2 power play only works if the wing rotates AFTER the pass. If they rotate early, the defense reads it and slides. Watch rotation timing Friday.
Watch For
  • Wing cuts with no eye contact: If the wing cuts before looking at the passer, the entry pass is late or forced. Cue: "Look first, then move."
  • Center-forward receives with weak top hand: Ball arrives, defender strips it immediately. Teach: top hand grips the stick at throat height, not down on the shaft.
  • Counter attack slows at half pool: Players sprint to center, then stop to "set up." No. Cue: "Read on the move — never stop sprinting until you shoot."
  • Press defenders chase passes instead of denying lanes: Defender swims after the ball, leaves their mark open. Cue: "Stay between your player and the ball. Don't chase."
  • GK outlets go long when a short pass is open: GK tries to be a hero. Reteach: short outlet to wing is safer and faster than a contested long ball.
  • Power-play rotation happens before the pass: Defense reads it, slides early, rotation creates no advantage. Cue: "Pass first. Rotate second."
