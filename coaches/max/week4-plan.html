Week 4 Focus — From Your Intake
Getting the ball up the field without turnovers — clearing with patience and purpose
Your offensive identity is transition-first — move the ball fast and attack before the defense sets. But you're asking your goalie to be patient on the clear, passing it around the back until the right moment. Same problem: players want to rush but don't know when "the right moment" is. They either go too fast (turnover at midfield) or too slow (the defense resets and you lose the advantage). This week resolves it by teaching the difference between a patient clear and a rushed transition — and naming the trigger to "go."
End of Week Outcome
Goalie calls "clear" (patient) or "push" (go fast) after every save. Wings know which lane to fill. Team crosses midfield with possession 7 out of 10 attempts by Friday's scrimmage.
Conditioning Foundation — 60-Min Session Structure
Every session: 8–10 min dynamic warm-up (high knees, butt kicks, carioca, backward run, A-skips, lateral shuffles) + partner passing (catch-look-pass, 25 reps each).
Tuesday: End-of-session 4 × 50-yard shuttle runs (cone at 25y; sprint out, backpedal back) · full recovery between reps.
Thursday: End-of-session 6 × 20-yard suicide sprints (0–10–20, touch each line) · 30s rest between reps.
All conditioning under fatigue — always at the end of the session, not the start. Hydration breaks every 15 minutes.
Goalie Track — Parallel Focus (first 20 min, every session)  ·  First-year goalie — position is new to them
All sessions: 5-min warm-up (partner passing with a field player, catches only — no clears yet).
Session 1 (Tuesday) — Stance & Ready Position: 15 min · teach athletic stance (knees bent, stick up, eyes on the ball) + lateral shuffle drill (side to side across the crease, 10 reps) + soft-toss catches from 10 yards (20 reps, mix high/low). Cue: "Knees bent, stick up, see the ball into your stick."
Session 2 (Thursday) — Clearing Fundamentals: 15 min · teach outlet pass mechanics (step toward target, push with back hand, follow through) + stationary outlet passes to a midfielder 15 yards away (15 reps) + live clear with one defender (goalie makes save, outlets to open midfielder, 10 reps). Cue: "Save, look, outlet — chest-level pass to the open player."
Goalie joins team for all clearing drills (Block 3, both sessions) and scrimmage (Block 5, both sessions). Coach watches: Does the goalie call "clear" or "push" after the save? Does the outlet pass reach the target?

Ground Ball Fundamentals — Noodle Scooping
Two lines at midfield. Coach rolls a ball out between the lines; first player from each line sprints to the ball, scoops through it (butt down, top hand down, scoop through the ball with a fish-hook pivot to shield the stick), carries full-field for a shot on cage. Reset, next pair. 10 reps per player. What this teaches: Ground balls are the foundation of possession — every lacrosse game is won or lost on the ratio of ground balls scooped cleanly.
Focal Noodle Scooping
25–40
4v3 Box Clearing — Patient Clear
Goalie + 3 defenders (two crease defenders, one midfielder) vs. no ride pressure. Goalie makes a "save" (coach feeds), calls "clear," outlets to a wing. Defenders spread wide across the defensive box. Ball swings: wing to crease defender to opposite wing to midfielder crossing midfield. 10 reps. Progression: add one passive rider at the top; if the rider pressures the ball, the nearest open player is the outlet. What this teaches: Clearing is how a stopped shot or saved shot becomes offense — every turnover prevented in the defensive half is worth more than a made shot.
Focal 4v3 Box
40–50
3v2 Fast Break — Push Transition
Goalie makes a save, calls "push," outlets to the near wing. Wing carries up the sideline lane, two midfielders fill the opposite lane and the middle. 3v2 numbers advantage to goal. Cue: "Fill the lanes, keep your width, first shot in 10 seconds." 8 reps. What this teaches: The advantage is only real if the ball moves fast — the defense can recover if the offense hesitates.
Transition 3v2 West Genny
50–60
Competitive 4v4 — Clear or Push Constraint
4v4 half-field. Goalie starts every possession. Constraint: Goalie must call "clear" or "push" out loud before the outlet pass. Offense must honor the call — if goalie calls "clear," must complete 3 passes before crossing midfield; if goalie calls "push," must cross midfield in under 6 seconds. Turnover = other team gets possession. Play to 5 goals or 8 minutes, whichever comes first.

Settled Offense + Transition

Arrival & Activation
Dynamic warm-up (same as Tuesday: high knees, butt kicks, carioca, backward run, A-skips, lateral shuffles). Partner passing: 25 reps each, add a pivot after every catch (catch, pivot to face the goal, then pass).
Warm-Up
10–22
3v3 Triangle Passing + Shot
Three attackmen in a triangle (one at X behind the goal, two on the wings). No defense. Pass around the triangle: X to wing to opposite wing back to X. After 5 passes, any player can dodge to the cage for a shot. 10 reps (rotate through all players). Cue: "Catch two hands, look at the goal, then pass." What this teaches: Settled offense is what happens when no one has a numbers advantage — the offense has to create an advantage by moving the ball and making the defense shift.
Focal Triangle Passing Drill
22–37
5v4 House Clearing — Live Pressure
4-man clear (goalie + 3 defenders) vs. 3-man hold-the-line ride, plus one extra floater midfielder who can support either the clear or the ride. Goalie makes save, calls "clear," outlets. Defenders must swing the ball back through the goalie if the first outlet is covered. Clear succeeds when the ball reaches the offensive box with possession. 10 reps. What this teaches: When the first outlet is covered, the safest play is to reset — swing it back through the goalie and look for the open man on the opposite side.
Focal 5v4 House
37–48
2-Man Game (Pick & Roll) — Live 2v2
Two offensive players (one ball carrier, one off-ball screener) vs. two defenders on the wing. Offensive player with the ball drives toward the defender; the screener sets a pick (feet set, stationary). Ball carrier reads the defense: if the defender fights over the screen, pass to the screener rolling to the cage; if the defender drops, keep the ball and shoot. 10 reps, rotate pairs. Cue: "Set the pick with feet set, roll to the ball." What this teaches: The pick-and-roll creates a two-on-one advantage for a split second — the offense has to read which defender is open and make the right pass.
Focal 2-Man Game (Pick & Roll)

COACHING NOTES
This Week's Priorities
  • Session 1 (Tuesday): Teach the goalie the two verbal calls — "clear" (patient, pass around the back) and "push" (go fast, fill the lanes). Practice the call in every clearing drill.
  • Session 1 (Tuesday): Wings learn their lanes — near-side wing is the first outlet on "push"; far-side wing fills the opposite lane on "push." On "clear," both wings spread wide across the box.
  • Session 2 (Thursday): Introduce the 2-man game (pick-and-roll) to attackmen. Keep it simple: set the pick, roll to the ball, read the defense.
  • Session 2 (Thursday): Settle the first-line clearing unit by jersey number. This group runs every clear in scrimmage and next week's sessions.
  • By Friday: The goalie's verbal call is automatic — no hesitation after the save. Team responds to the call, not the situation. Track: how many clears succeed vs. how many possessions start with a turnover at midfield.
Evaluation Notes
  • Goalie outlet accuracy: Does the outlet pass reach the target midfielder or wing? If not, is it a technical issue (pass too high, too low) or a decision issue (wrong outlet, covered player)?
  • Clear success location: Track where clears succeed or fail — at the outlet (goalie to defender), at midfield (defender to midfielder), or in transition (midfielder to attackman). Most turnovers at this level happen at midfield because the midfielder carries too long.
  • Transition shot speed: On "push" clears, how long does it take from the goalie save to the first shot? Target: under 12 seconds. If it's over 15, the transition advantage is gone.
  • Ground ball effort: Count how many ground balls each player pursues vs. how many they scoop. Effort shows up before technique at this level.
  • Settled offense patience: When the ball crosses midfield on a "clear" possession, does the offense take a bad shot early (under 8 seconds) or do they pass until a good look appears? Track shot quality (inside finish, open look, contested rush).
  • Pick-and-roll reads: On 2-man game reps, does the ball carrier read the defense or force the pass? If the defender drops, the ball carrier should keep it and shoot; if the defender fights over, pass to the roller.
Watch For
  • Goalie hesitates on the call: If the goalie doesn't call "clear" or "push" within 2 seconds of the save, the clearing unit doesn't know what to do. Re-drill the call in warm-up next session.
  • Wings don't spread on "clear": On patient clears, if the wings stay bunched near midfield instead of spreading wide across the box, the outlets are covered. Cue: "Wings wide — give the goalie two options."
  • Midfielder carries too long: The most common turnover at this level: the midfielder receives the outlet, carries up the middle, and gets stripped at midfield. Cue: "Two touches — catch and pass, or catch and dodge to space."
  • Attackmen ball-watch on clears: When the ball is still in the defensive box, attackmen often stand and watch instead of finding their starting position in the offensive box. Cue: "Clear your space — move before the ball gets there."
  • Pick-and-roll screener moves early: The pick is only legal if the screener's feet are set when contact happens. If the screener is still moving, it's a moving pick (penalty). Cue: "Set your feet, hold for one second, then roll."
  • Players call for the ball but don't move: On settled offense, players yell "I'm open!" but don't cut or dodge to create separation. Cue: "If you want the ball, move to an open space — don't stand and call."
