Week 1 Focus — From Your Intake
When the other team adjusts and I don't know how to respond
This week builds the foundation: teach stick skills and ground balls so every player can execute the basics under pressure. When individual skills are clean, team adjustments become decisions, not chaos.
End of Week Outcome
Every player can scoop a ground ball cleanly, catch and pass with two hands, and carry the ball while being chased — the three skills that solve 80% of first-year problems.

Session Breakdown
Monday
Stick Skills
Priority: Every player leaves knowing how to hold the stick and where their hands go
0–8
Arrival & Activation
Dynamic warm-up: high knees, butt kicks, side shuffles, carioca. Partner up. Partner Passing — stand 5 yards apart, pass and catch with two hands on the stick. Focus: "Catch two hands, look at your partner, then pass."
Warm-up
8–18
Stick Foundation — Hand Cradling
No sticks yet. Each player uses their hands to cradle an imaginary ball. Coach demos: bottom hand at belly button, top hand out in front like holding a flashlight. Rock side-to-side. Add walking, then light jogging. Teach the word "cradle" — it means rocking the stick to keep the ball in the pocket.
Focal · Hand Cradling Hand Cradling
18–35
Ground Balls — Noodle Scooping
Two lines. Coach rolls a ball out between them. Player runs to ball, scoops through it (butt down, top hand down, scoop through the ball), and carries it in a fish-hook turn away from pressure. Rotate. Cue every rep: "Butt down, top hand down, scoop through it."
Focal · Noodle Scooping Noodle Scooping
35–45
Small-Sided Play — Messy Backyard
Scatter 8–10 balls in a 20×20 yard grid. On whistle, every player runs to scoop a ball, carries it to a cone, drops it, and goes back for another. First player to bring 3 balls to their cone wins. Constraint: must scoop through every ball — no picking it up with hands.
Game-Based Messy Backyard
45–50
Cool-Down & Message
Walk lap around the field. Teach point: "Ground balls are the most important skill in lacrosse. If you can scoop the ball, you can play." Player callout: name one player who kept their butt down on every scoop. Water break.
Decision Identify your top 3 ground-ball players by name — they start every GB battle in Wednesday's session.
Wednesday
Catching & Passing
Priority: Every pass starts and ends with two hands on the stick
0–8
Arrival & Activation
Dynamic warm-up (same as Monday). Partner Passing — 5 yards, two hands, focus on soft hands. Cue: "Catch with your eyes — see the ball into the pocket."
Warm-up
8–18
Passing Foundation — Water Balloon Toss
Partners stand 3 yards apart. Soft toss, catch with two hands. Slowly increase distance to 5 yards. If a pass is dropped, both players take one step closer. Cue: "Push with the back foot, step with the front." Teach the word "overhand pass" — a pass where the stick comes over the shoulder.
Focal · Water Balloon Toss Water Balloon Toss
18–35
Ground Balls — 1v1 GB's
1v1 box drill. Two players face each other, loose ball between them. On whistle, both sprint to scoop. Winner carries to a cone. Loser chases but does not check. Cue: "Box out with your hips, then scoop away from pressure." Your top 3 GB players from Monday go first every round.
Focal · 1v1 GB's 1v1 GB's
35–45
Small-Sided Play — Catch if You Can
4v4 in a 30×30 yard grid. One ball. Objective: complete 5 consecutive passes without a drop. Defense plays light pressure — hands behind back, just chase. Constraint: every catch must use two hands. If the ball hits the ground, count resets to zero.
Game-Based Catch if You Can
45–50
Cool-Down & Message
Walk lap. Teach point: "Two hands on every catch means you control the ball. One hand = ball on the ground." Player callout: name one player whose passes were always catchable. Water break.
Decision Pick 4 players who consistently catch with two hands — they are your first-line fast-clear unit for Friday.

5–15
Ground Ball Competition — Hungry Hippos
Four teams in four corners of a grid. One ball in the center. On whistle, one player from each team sprints to scoop. Winner's team gets a point. Rotate players every rep. First team to 5 points wins. Cue: "Scoop through it, then carry it home."

15–25
Fast Clear Intro — 4v3 Box
Goalie (or coach with stick) + 3 field players. No ride. Goalie scoops a simulated save, looks to a wing, passes. Wing catches, carries across midline. Rotate. Teach: "Goalie looks wing first; field players spread wide." Your first-line clearing unit runs this 10 times.

25–40
Live Scrimmage — 4v4 Small-Sided
Two small fields (no full-field play at U10). 4v4 with rolling subs. Constraint: every possession must include at least one pass before a shot. Whistle every 2 minutes, rotate players. Coach watches for: ground ball technique, stick protection, two-hand catches.
Live Play
40–45

Circle up. Ask: "Who had fun today?" (hands up). Teach point: "This week you learned the three most important skills: scoop the ball, catch with two hands, protect your stick. That's lacrosse." Player callout: name one player who improved the most from Monday to Friday. Water, high-fives, done.

Focal Drills — Full Detail
Drill Setup Reps/Duration Coaching Cue What This Teaches Progression
Hand Cradling
Teaches players the cradling motion before adding the complexity of a stick.
 No equipment. Players stand in two lines. Coach demonstrates cradling motion with hands only. 10 min · 3 rounds "Bottom hand at your belly button, top hand out in front like a flashlight. Rock side-to-side." Cradling is how you keep the ball in the pocket when you run — this drill isolates the motion so players feel it in their body first.
Entry
Standing still, rocking hands side-to-side
Full
Walking, then jogging while cradling
Noodle Scooping
Teaches the fundamental ground-ball technique: butt down, top hand down, scoop through the ball.
 Two lines facing each other, 10 yards apart. Coach stands in the middle and rolls a ball out. 15 min · 20 reps per player "Butt down, top hand down, scoop through it." Ground balls decide possession. If you can scoop cleanly, your team gets the ball. If you can't, the other team does.
Entry
Coach rolls ball slowly; player scoops with no pressure
Full
Coach rolls ball fast; player must scoop and fish-hook away from imaginary defender
Water Balloon Toss
Teaches soft hands and two-hand catching — the foundation of every pass.
 Partners stand 3 yards apart. Soft toss back and forth. Slowly increase distance to 5 yards. 10 min · 40 catches per player "See the ball into the pocket. Soft hands — the ball is an egg." If you can catch with two hands, you control the ball. One-hand catches lead to drops and turnovers.
Entry
3 yards, underhand toss, stationary
Full
5 yards, overhand pass, both players moving toward each other
1v1 GB's
Teaches ground-ball technique under light pressure — the first step toward game situations.
 1v1 box drill. Two players face each other 5 yards apart. Loose ball in the middle. 12 min · 10 reps per player "Box out with your hips, then scoop away from pressure." In a game, ground balls are contested. This drill teaches players to protect their space before they scoop.
Entry
Light pressure — defender chases but does not check
Full
Full pressure — defender can check after the scoop (U12+)
4v3 Box
Teaches the first step of clearing: goalie outlets to a wing, wing carries across midline.
 Half-field. Goalie + 3 field players. No ride. Goalie starts with ball, looks to wing, passes. 10 min · 15 reps "Goalie looks wing first; field players spread wide." Clearing is how you turn a save into offense. This drill teaches the goalie-to-wing outlet — the safest first pass.
Entry
No ride, walk-through the outlet pass
Full
Add 2 riders to force decision-making (U12+)
Hungry Hippos
Teaches ground-ball speed and competitiveness in a game-based format.
 Four teams in four corners of a 20×20 yard grid. One ball in the center. 10 min · 20 rounds "Scoop through it, then carry it home." Ground balls are races. This drill teaches players to sprint to the ball, scoop cleanly, and get out fast.
Entry
One player per team, clean scoop wins
Full
Two players per team — winner must pass to teammate before scoring a point
Coaching Notes
This Week's Priorities
  • Monday: Teach the grip and the cradle. Players should leave knowing where their hands go on the stick.
  • Wednesday: Teach two-hand catching. Every catch in every drill must use both hands on the stick.
  • Friday: Let them play. The scrimmage is the reward for two days of fundamentals.
  • Teaching sequence: Demo the skill slowly. Walk through it with the group. Then let them try it. Correct one player at a time — don't stop the whole group for one mistake.
  • Ground balls are the priority. If a player can scoop the ball cleanly, they can play lacrosse. Everything else is secondary this week.
Evaluation Notes
  • Watch ground-ball technique every rep: butt down, top hand down, scoop through. Log which players bend at the waist (common mistake).
  • Track two-hand catches during Partner Passing and Water Balloon Toss. If a player catches one-handed, cue immediately: "Two hands — both hands on the stick."
  • During scrimmage, watch for players who protect their stick when chased (top hand out in front). Count how many do it naturally vs. how many need reminders.
  • Track pass completion rate during small-sided games. If below 60%, the distance is too far or the pace is too fast.
  • Watch for players standing idle. If more than 4 players are waiting in line at any check, split the group smaller.
  • Ask 3 random players after Friday: "Did you have fun today?" If any player says no, adjust Week 2 to include more game-based drills and fewer lines.
Watch For (U10 Failure Modes)
  • Players bend at the waist instead of dropping the hips on GBs. Cue: "Butt down" every rep until it sticks.
  • Ball-carrier carries with the top hand on the plastic (choked up). Cue: "Hand down the shaft — slide it toward the middle."
  • Players catching one-handed. Stop the drill and reset: "Two hands, always."
  • Players throwing passes too hard. Cue: "Soft hands — the ball is an egg. Your partner should catch it without the ball bouncing out."
  • Players running after scooping a GB instead of fish-hooking away from pressure. Demo the fish-hook every session until it becomes automatic.
  • Goalies standing flat-footed. Teach: "Knees bent, hands ready, eyes on the ball."
Week 2 Trigger
If 2 or more KPIs fall below target by the Friday session → repeat the Week 1 focal theme in Week 2 with reduced complexity. Add no new concepts until ground balls, two-hand catches, and stick protection under pressure are consistent.
