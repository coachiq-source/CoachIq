Week 1 Focus — From Your Intake
Attack depth and transition confusion — players reacting instead of reading the field
Your roster is thin at attack, and you prioritize transition-first offense, but players are guessing instead of reading the field during fast clears. Same root cause as freezing during late-game situations: players reacting to what's in front of them instead of reading the full picture. This week establishes spatial awareness, transition lanes, and basic decision-making under pressure — the foundation for attacking before the defense sets.
End of Week Outcome
Players recognize transition lanes and attack windows before the defense sets. Three transition counter attacks executed in Friday scrimmage with correct outlet reads. Players verbalize "outlet left / outlet right / hold" before the GK releases the ball.
Swim Conditioning — 25y Pool · Dante Philosophy
All sets: head-up · no walls · 1:1 work:rest · two groups (Group 2 leaves when Group 1 touches far end). Time every rep.
Mon / Thu: 10 × 25y | Tue: 6 × 25y · full recovery | Week 2: add 2 reps Mon/Thu.
GK Track — Parallel Focus (0–15 min)  ·  Position unsettled — building fundamentals
All days: 100y warm-up (25 free / 25 backstroke / 25 eggbeater / 25 vertical backstroke) + 2 × 50y technical block (GP hold · Lateral movement · Quick Hands).
Mon / Thu (Day 1 — Positional): 3 × 5-min blocks · 20s work / 10s rest (GP hold · Near-side glide · Cross-cage glide).
Tue (Activation only): 100y warm-up + 1 × 50y technical set. Joins team for all transition reps.
GK communication priority: Call "outlet left / outlet right / hold" on every counter attack rep — louder when shape breaks, not quieter.

Ground Ball Recovery

GK Outlet Decision
Track GK outlet calls — correct read vs. rushed release

Late-Game Composure
Track timeout clarity — did coach verbalize 2 clear instructions?
If timeout stalls 2+ times Friday → script timeout sequence before Week 2
Zone Defense Shape
Count shape breaks during 6x5 reps — player name + moment
If same player breaks shape 4+ times → add positional drill in Week 2
Week 2 Adjustment
If 2 or more KPIs fall below target at Friday scrimmage → repeat the Week 1 focal theme in Week 2 with reduced complexity. Add no new concepts until transition lane reads, GK outlet calls, and ground ball recovery are consistent under live pressure.

Drill Setup Reps/Duration Coaching Cue Progression Success Metric
Counter Attack Lanes (Create / Read / Attack) GK at goal. 3 field players at opposite end. GK starts with ball. 12 reps · rotate GK every 4 "Call outlet before you release — players create lanes on your voice"
Entry level
GK holds ball 3 seconds, field players swim slowly to create visible lanes. GK calls outlet when lanes are clear.
Full version
Add one defender chasing. GK must call outlet before defender recovers. Field players sprint-swim to create separation.

Advantage Rules (2v1) Two attackers, one defender. Start at half pool. Ball with trailing attacker. 15 reps · rotate groups every 5 "Attack before the defense recovers — read the defender's position, not the goal"
Entry level
Defender starts at 5-meter line. Attackers have full advantage. Emphasize outlet pass accuracy.
Full version
Defender starts at 2-meter line, sprints to recover. Attackers must attack within 5 seconds or defender resets advantage.
 10/15 reps converted to shot on goal
Pressure Passing (Wet & Dry) Two lines facing each other, 5 yards apart. Ball with one line. Dry: 2 × 20 reps · Wet: 2 × 15 reps "Dry pass: hands ready, ball to chest. Wet pass: no ball drop, eggbeater stable"
Entry level
Dry pass only. Stationary. Focus on hand position and follow-through. No defensive pressure.
Full version
Wet pass with eggbeater. Add passive defender on rep 10+. Defender raises hand but does not block.
 Wet reps: 12/15 clean catches, no ball drop
Monkey in the Middle (Zone Defense Intro) Three attackers in triangle, one defender in center. Ball starts with any attacker. 4 × 90s blocks · rotate defender every block "Defender: Ball / Player / Area — call it before every pass. Hold your position, force outside"
Entry level
Attackers pass slowly. Defender holds center position, does not lunge. Emphasize verbal call.
Full version
Attackers pass at game speed. Defender can lunge to pressure but must recover to center. Add shot fake on rep 3 of each block.
 Defender verbalizes call 8+ times per 90s block
3-3 Primary Look (6x5 Intro) Three attackers (wing, flat, wing) vs. two defenders. GK in goal. Ball starts at 2-meter line. 12 reps · rotate groups every 4 "Move the ball, find the open shooter. GK calls shape before every rep"
Entry level
Defenders hold stationary positions. Attackers pass 3+ times before shooting. GK calls shape, attackers look for open passing lane.
Full version
Defenders shift with ball movement. Attackers must attack within 15 seconds. GK calls shape break if defenders collapse.
 8/12 reps result in open shot or clear passing lane
Spidering (Foundational) Players in shallow water, hands on pool deck. Body horizontal, legs extended behind. 3 × 30s · full recovery between reps "Core tight, legs together, breathe steady — this is your foundation position"
Entry level
Hold static position. Knees can touch bottom if needed. Focus on body alignment and breathing.
Full version
Lift knees off bottom. Add 5-second flutter kick every 10 seconds of hold. Maintain horizontal alignment throughout.
 All players hold 25+ seconds per rep
Coaching Notes
Week 1 Priorities
  • Establish transition lane awareness before adding speed — players must see outlets before they execute
  • GK communication is non-negotiable — every outlet must have a verbal call (left/right/hold)
  • Zone defense fundamentals come before pressure — hold position first, then add movement
  • This team is in the fundamentals phase — every drill starts at the entry level before progressing
  • Late-game composure starts with timeout clarity — script 2 clear instructions before Friday scrimmage
Scrimmage / Evaluation Notes
  • Log outlet calls by player name — track who reads left/right/hold correctly vs. who guesses
  • Count counter attack conversions vs. attempts — if below 50%, slow down outlet release
  • Track zone shape breaks by player name and moment — same player breaking shape 3+ times needs positional drill
  • Watch GK decision-making under pressure — rushed outlets vs. correct reads
  • Ground ball recovery by player name — if same 3 players dominate, add ground ball drill in Week 2
  • Note timeout clarity — did coach verbalize 2 clear instructions? Did players execute?
Watch For
  • Players guessing outlet direction instead of reading GK call — if persistent, add visual cue (GK points before calling)
  • Counter attack speed exceeding reading ability — slow down, add "hold" call before release
  • Zone defenders collapsing to ball instead of holding area — reset "Ball / Player / Area" mantra
  • GK releasing ball before field players create lanes — GK must hold 2–3 seconds longer
  • Late-game panic (freezing) — if coach freezes in timeout, script sequence before Week 2
  • Fatigue overwhelming skill execution — if players lose form after 30 min, reduce swim volume
