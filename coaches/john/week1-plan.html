Week 1 Focus — From Your Intake
Ground balls, stick skills, and learning what the field looks like.
This is a brand-new program with mostly beginner players and a first-year coach. The job this week is simple: teach what a lacrosse stick does, what a ground ball is, and how to move the ball up the field safely. Every drill stays at the Introduction and Exploration stages — no scheme, no complexity, just fundamentals repeated until they feel normal.
End of Week Outcome
By the end of this week, every player can cradle while jogging, scoop a rolling ball without stopping their feet, and complete a catch-and-throw sequence with a partner from 5 yards. The team understands that ground balls decide games, and that moving the ball is safer than carrying it alone.
Conditioning — Age-Appropriate Movement Foundation
U10 girls (45-min sessions): No dedicated conditioning block. All movement happens inside games and drills. Each session begins with a 5-minute dynamic warm-up (high knees, butt kicks, side shuffles, carioca — all at half speed, laughing encouraged). The warm-up teaches how to move on a field, not how to get tired. Water breaks every 10 minutes.
Goalie Track — Position Unsettled  ·  This week identifies goalie candidates through live reps
Session 1: No goalie. All shooting drills feed to an empty net with a coach standing in the crease to demonstrate positioning. Players rotate through the crease to see what the goalie sees.
Session 2: Invite any interested player to try goalie for one 10-minute block (the small-sided game). Provide a chest protector if available; otherwise a player can try it in regular gear. The goal is exposure, not evaluation. Coach stands next to the goalie and narrates every shot: "That one was high — you track it with your eyes and raise your stick." No pressure, just learning.
Girls' lacrosse goalie note: The draw (girls' face-off) happens at midfield, not in the crease. Goalie does not participate in the draw. After a goal, goalie restarts play from inside the crease with a self-clear or a pass to a defender.
WEEK 1 TRACKING PRIORITIES
Ground Ball Attempts
Count attempts, not wins — track how many players go for the ball vs. how many watch
If fewer than half the team goes for ground balls in live play → add one more ground-ball drill to Session 2
Catch Success Rate
Partner passing — count catches per 10 throws (target: 6/10 by end of week)
If below 5/10 after Session 1 → slow down throwing speed and shorten distance to 3 yards in Session 2
Cradling While Moving
Watch for top hand position — is it sliding down to the plastic, or staying on the shaft?
If more than half the team chokes up on the stick → add a "hand-check" cue to every cradle drill
Listen-and-Look
Can players hear a whistle and stop within 2 seconds?
If players don't stop on the whistle → practice whistle-stop 3 times at the start of Session 2
Smiles Per Session
Informal — are players laughing, asking questions, coming back for more?
If energy drops or players look confused → shorten the drill and add a water break
Goalie Interest
Track how many players volunteer to try goalie in Session 2
If zero volunteers → assign rotation for Week 2 (everyone tries it once, 5 min each)
Week 2 Adjustment Trigger
If catch success rate stays below 5/10 AND players are still stopping their feet to scoop ground balls at the first game → repeat Week 1's focal theme in Week 2 with no new concepts. Add clearing and 3v2 only after stick skills and ground balls are consistent under live pressure.

SESSION BREAKDOWN
Session 1
Stick Skills
Priority: Every player holds the stick correctly and completes one clean catch by the end of the session
0–5
Arrival + Dynamic Warm-Up
High knees, butt kicks, side shuffles, carioca (each 10 yards, half speed). Teach the movements, not the intensity. Finish with two laps around the field at a jog — sticks on the ground, no carrying yet.
Warm-Up
5–15
Stick Introduction + Hand Cradling
Every player gets a stick. Teach the parts: head, pocket, shaft, butt. Show top-hand position (halfway down the shaft, thumb up) and bottom-hand position (butt end, pinky down). Cradle demo: rock the stick ear-to-ear, soft hands. Then Hand Cradling (no ball): players cradle an imaginary ball while walking in a circle. Cue: "Top hand stays on the shaft — don't slide it down to the plastic."
Focal Drill Hand Cradling
15–25
Coach Toss + Partner Passing Intro
Coach Toss (5 min): Coach tosses a ball underhand to each player from 3 yards. Player catches in their stick (box position — top hand at armpit, stick vertical). Cue: "See the ball into the pocket." 5 reps each.

Partner Passing (5 min): Pairs, 5 yards apart. Catch first, then throw. Cue: "Catch two hands, look at your partner, then pass." Count successful catches (target: 3/10 to start — anything caught is a win).
Focal Drill Coach Toss Partner Passing
25–35
Noodle Scooping (Ground Ball Introduction)
Two lines facing the coach. Coach rolls a ball out. Player runs to it, scoops through it (butt down, top hand down, scoop through the ball), and jogs away with a fish-hook turn. Cue: "Butt down, top hand down, scoop through it." No defense, no pressure — just the motion. 8 reps each. Celebrate every clean scoop out loud.
Focal Drill Noodle Scooping
35–43
3v3 Handball (Small-Sided Introduction)
Two teams of 3, small field (20 yards). No sticks — hands only. Goal: pass the ball across the end line. Teaches spacing, passing, and moving without the stick. No tackling, no contact — just passing and running. If a team scores, they get the ball back. Play until time.
Live Play
43–45
Cool-Down + Message
Two laps around the field at a walk. Gather in a circle. Message: "You all held a stick today. That's lacrosse. Next time we add a ball and see what happens. One player callout: [name the player who had the best attitude or tried the hardest]. Water."
Decision Identify your top 3 ground-ball players by name — they demonstrate Noodle Scooping at the start of Session 2.
Session 2
Ground Balls + Clearing
Priority: Every player goes for at least one ground ball in live play, and the team completes one successful clear (ball crosses midfield)
0–5
Arrival + Dynamic Warm-Up
Same as Session 1: high knees, butt kicks, side shuffles, carioca (10 yards each, half speed). Add one new movement: backward jog (10 yards). Two laps around the field at a jog — sticks in hand this time, cradling while jogging (no ball yet).
Warm-Up
5–12
Form Cradling + Partner Passing Review
Form Cradling (3 min): Players walk in a circle with a ball in their stick, cradling ear-to-ear. Cue: "Top hand halfway down, bottom hand at the butt." Coach walks around and hand-checks (gently taps the stick — if it falls out, grip is too loose).

Partner Passing (4 min): Same as Session 1, but now 7 yards apart. Count catches per 10 throws. Target: 5/10.
Form Cradling Partner Passing
12–22
1v1 GB's (Ground Ball Battle)
Two players face each other, 5 yards apart. Coach rolls a ball between them. Both go for it. Winner scoops and jogs away. Cue: "Box out with your hips, then scoop away from pressure." No stick checks — hips only. 6 reps each. What this teaches: Ground balls are contested — you have to want it more than the other player.
Focal Drill 1v1 GB's
22–32
4v3 Box (Clearing Introduction)
Goalie + 3 defenders (no goalie if position unsettled — use a coach). No ride (no defense pressuring the clear). Goalie passes to a defender, defenders pass to each other and carry the ball over the midfield line. Cue: "Goalie looks wide first; defenders spread out." What this teaches: Clearing is how you turn a save into offense — spread wide, pass safe, don't force it. Target: 7/10 clears cross midfield.
Focal Drill 4v3 Box
32–42
3v3 Short Field (Live Play with Constraint)
Two teams of 3, 30 yards long. Constraint: every team must complete 2 passes before shooting. Goalie optional (or coach in the crease). Restart after a goal with a goalie clear or a coach toss to the defensive team. What this teaches: Passing is safer than dodging — move the ball, not just your feet.
Live Play
42–45
Cool-Down + Message
Two laps at a walk. Gather. Message: "You went for ground balls today. That's how games are won. Next time is our first game — we're going to play, have fun, and do what we practiced. One player callout: [name]. Water."
Decision Name your starting goalie for the first game (or decide to rotate every quarter if position is still unsettled).

COACHING NOTES
This Week's Priorities
  • Session 1: Teach stick parts, hand position, and the cradle motion. Success = every player can cradle while walking.
  • Session 1: Introduce catching with Coach Toss. Target: 3/10 catches. Anything caught is celebrated.
  • Session 1: Introduce the ground ball scoop with Noodle Scooping. No pressure, no defense — just the motion.
  • Session 2: Add contested ground balls (1v1 GB's). Teach that ground balls are fought for, not given.
  • Session 2: Introduce clearing with 4v3 Box. The job is to get the ball past midfield safely — no hero plays.
First Game Notes
  • Expect chaos. The other team has practiced too — they might have practiced more. That's fine. Your job is to keep your players smiling.
  • Watch ground balls: do your players go for them, or do they watch? Log who goes for the ball and who hesitates.
  • Watch passing: are players trying to pass, or are they all carrying alone? If they're carrying, remind them between quarters: "Pass first, carry second."
  • Watch the draw (girls' face-off at midfield): does your team know to run to the ball after the whistle? If not, practice it once before the second quarter.
  • Watch your goalie (if you have one): is she tracking the ball with her eyes, or looking at her feet? Cue her once per quarter: "Eyes on the ball."
  • Log who scores, who assists, and who asks to try goalie. Those are your leaders.
Watch For (U10 Beginner Failure Modes)
  • Players bend at the waist instead of dropping their hips on ground balls. Cue: "Butt down" every rep.
  • Top hand slides down to the plastic during cradling. Hand-check every player in Session 1 and Session 2.
  • Players throw before they look. Every bad pass is a "look first" cue, not a "throw harder" cue.
  • Players stop their feet to scoop a ground ball. The feet never stop — scoop on the run, fish-hook away.
  • Players crowd the ball in the small-sided game. Teach spacing with a verbal cue: "Spread out — give your teammate room."
  • Players get frustrated when they drop a pass. Celebrate effort ("You went for it — that's lacrosse") and move on. Frustration kills fun.
First-Year Coach Note
Timeout management (your stated gap): You have two timeouts per half in girls' U10 lacrosse. Use the first one when something breaks (players stop passing, or the other team scores 3 in a row). Script: "We're fine. Pass the ball. Find the open player. Go." That's it. Use the second timeout with 2 minutes left if you're winning (to rest your best players) or if you're losing (to remind them it's about fun, not the score). The timeout isn't about strategy at U10 — it's about calming the chaos and giving them one thing to remember.
