Same problem: players are running patterns instead of reading the game. This week resolves it by teaching ONE simple backup decision every player can make when the first look is covered — look for the open space, not the called play.

Goalie Track — Entry-Level Foundation (parallel to team, 0–15 min)
Both sessions: Goalie position is unsettled — rotate 2–3 players through the crease each session. Focus: stick positioning (stick up, top hand at shoulder), stance (knees bent, weight on balls of feet), and tracking the ball (eyes follow every pass). Drill: Coach feeds 10 soft shots from 8 meters, goalie calls "ball" on every shot and gets stick to it. Emphasis on effort and courage, not saves. Goalies join all scrimmage blocks.

FOCAL DRILLS — FULL DETAIL
Drill Setup Reps/Duration Coaching Cue What This Teaches Progression
Partner Passing (Box Position)
Players learn to receive the ball in the safest, most protected position — stick vertical, top hand at armpit.
 Two lines, 10 yards apart. Partners face each other. Ball starts with one side. 10 min · 10 consecutive catches per pair "Box position — top hand at the armpit, stick vertical. See the ball into the pocket." Receiving a pass correctly is the foundation of keeping possession — if you can't catch it clean, you can't do anything else.
Entry: Stationary passing, both players standing still. No defense.
Full: Passing while moving (jog toward each other, pass, turn, repeat).
Noodle Scooping
Ground balls are the foundation of possession — this drill teaches the safest way to pick up a loose ball without losing it.
 Two lines, coach rolls a ball out between them. Player scoops through, fish-hooks out (turn away from pressure). 10 min · 8–10 reps per player "Butt down, top hand down, scoop through it. Feet never stop." Every lacrosse game is won or lost on ground balls — the team that scoops more wins.
Entry: Coach rolls the ball slowly, one player at a time, no pressure.
Full: Two players race to the ball (1v1 GB's), winner scoops and clears out.
3v2 Handball (Backup Decision)
When the first pass is covered, players must turn and find open space instead of forcing a bad pass — this is the backup decision.
 3 offensive players, 2 defenders, half-field. Offense must complete 2 passes before shooting. 10 min · 6–8 reps "If your pass is covered, turn and find open space. Don't force it." Most turnovers at this level come from forcing passes into traffic — the backup decision is: turn, look for space, reset.
Entry: Walk through the first 3 reps. Defense plays light pressure, no stick checks. Coach pauses play to cue "Is your pass covered?"
Full: Live 3v2 with active defense. Offense must identify covered passes on their own.
Form Cradling + Zig Zag Cradling
Cradling keeps the ball in the stick while you run — if you can't cradle, you can't carry the ball up the field.
 Players start in place (form cradling), then run a zig-zag pattern through cones (20 yards). 10 min · 5–6 reps per player "Rock the stick, don't squeeze it. Top hand does the work." Carrying the ball under control is how you create fast-break opportunities — every player must be able to cradle 20 yards without dropping it.
Entry: Cradle in place (form cradling) — no movement. Focus on the rocking motion.
Full: Cradle while running full speed in a straight line, then add zig-zag direction changes.
1v1 Ground Balls
In a game, ground balls are contested — you have to box out the opponent and scoop away from pressure.
 1v1 box drill. Coach rolls a loose ball between two players. Players race to the ball, box out, scoop. 10 min · 8–10 reps per player "Box out with your hips, then scoop away from pressure." Winning contested ground balls is about body position first, stick work second — get your hips between the ball and the opponent.
Entry: One player starts closer to the ball (advantage drill) — focus on scoop mechanics under light pressure.
Full: Both players start equidistant. Winner scoops and completes an outlet pass to a third player.
4v3 Box (Fast Clear)
Clearing is how you turn defense into offense — the faster you push it up the field, the more likely you are to catch the other team out of position.
 Goalie + 3 defenders, 4 offensive players. No ride (other team drops back). Goal: reach the offensive box in under 10 seconds. 10 min · 6–8 reps "Goalie looks for the outlet right away. Defenders spread wide. Push it up — don't wait." Fast clears create transition opportunities — the other team can't set their defense if you move the ball before they're ready.
Entry: No ride. Goalie outlets to a wing, wing carries up the sideline, swing middle at midfield.
Full: Add a 3-player ride (light pressure). Clear must still reach the box in under 10 seconds.
COACHING NOTES
Week 2 Priorities
  • Install the backup decision: "If my pass is covered, I turn and look for open space." Every player must be able to say it out loud.
  • Lock in box position on catches — top hand at the armpit, stick vertical. This is the safest receiving position and prevents stick checks.
  • Ground ball fundamentals: butt down, top hand down, scoop through it. Feet never stop. Fish-hook out (turn away from pressure).
  • Protect what's working: fast clears are a strength. Keep cueing "push it up" — don't let players settle into slow possessions.
  • Girls' lacrosse shooting space: remind players that if a defender is in the lane between them and the goal, they can't shoot. Turn and find a new angle.
Scrimmage / Evaluation Notes
  • Count by name: players who execute the backup decision (turn and find space when first pass is covered) without a coach cue. Target: 6+ by Thu.
  • Track ground ball wins by player name. Identify the top 3 — they become your GB captains and demonstrate at the start of Week 3.
  • Watch for players who catch 10 in a row in partner passing. This is your "catch-certified" list — aim for 10+ players by end of Week 2.
  • Log fast-clear attempts that reach the offensive box in under 10 seconds. If you're getting 4+ per scrimmage, your transition game is ahead of schedule.
  • Goalie courage moments: count shots faced, not saves. We're building confidence — every shot faced is a win.
  • Watch for players who force passes into traffic. This is the #1 turnover source at U10 — the backup decision fixes it.
Watch For — U10 Girls' Lacrosse
  • Players bend at the waist instead of dropping the hips on ground balls. Cue: "Butt down" every rep.
  • Ball-carrier carries with the top hand on the plastic (choked up). Cue: "Hand down the shaft" — top hand should be 6–8 inches from the head.
  • Players lock onto the corner and telegraph the shot. Cue: "Look at the goal, not the corner you want."
  • On passes, players step with the wrong foot (back foot instead of front). Cue: "Push with the back foot, step with the front."
  • Players squeeze the stick instead of rocking it. Cue: "Hold it, but don't squeeze it" — soft hands on the cradle.
  • Shooting space violations (girls' lacrosse): players shoot when a defender is in the lane. Stop play, explain the rule, reset. This is a learning year.
