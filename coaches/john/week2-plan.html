Week 2 Focus — From Your Week 1 Review
6x5 conversion stalled when the primary look is taken away — players are guessing instead of reading the second option
Week 1 showed strong counter-attack speed — that's working. But 6x5 regressed because players don't know what to do when the first look is denied. This week resolves it by teaching a simple read progression: if the first look is covered, look for the second option instead of forcing a bad pass or holding the ball too long.
End of Week 2 Outcome
Players can name the second 6x5 look out loud before the whistle. Counter-attack speed stays protected — no new counter concepts this week. First game on May 3 features clean 6x5 reads under pressure.
CONDITIONING BAR — U10 AGE-APPROPRIATE
Session A: 5 min dynamic warm-up · partner passing while jogging (cradle-catch-pass) · stick in motion the whole time.
Session B: 5 min dynamic warm-up · 4 Corners Pass drill (stationary passing around the horn) · catch-look-pass rhythm.
GOALIE TRACK — Parallel Focus (First 10 min of each session)  ·  Goalie position unsettled — use both sessions to evaluate candidates
Both sessions: Goalie candidates work with an assistant or parent volunteer on basic positioning and catching. Focus: See the ball into the stick (soft hands, track the ball all the way). No live shots yet — coach tosses or rolls the ball from 5 yards. Candidates rotate every 3 min so all interested players get reps. By end of Session B, name your starting goalie for the May 3 game.

Week 3 Adjustment Trigger

Add no new team concepts until second-look reads and ground-ball fundamentals are consistent in live 3v2 pressure.

FOCAL DRILLS — FULL DETAIL
Drill Setup Reps/Duration Coaching Cue What This Teaches Progression
Partner Passing Partners 5 yards apart, facing each other. 10 min
10 consecutive completions per pair		 "Catch two hands, look at the goal, then pass." The foundation of all lacrosse — you can't play without being able to catch and pass.
Entry
Stationary, 5 yards, no pressure. Box position every catch (top hand at armpit, stick vertical).
Full
On the move, 10 yards, passing while jogging. Emphasize lead passes (pass where they're going, not where they are).
Noodle Scooping Two lines, coach rolls ball out between them. 10 min
8 reps per player		 "Butt down, top hand down, scoop through it." Ground balls are the foundation of possession — every game is won or lost on the ratio of GBs scooped cleanly.
Entry
Coach rolls the ball, player scoops through, fish-hooks out. No defenders. Target: clean scoop, feet never stop.
Full
1v1 GB — two players race to the ball. Winner scoops, loser plays defense. Introduce body positioning (box out with hips before scooping).
3v2 Handball 3 attackers, 2 defenders (no sticks on defense to start). Ball starts at top. 10 min
10 reps		 "First look: wing. Second look: swing back, look opposite wing." This is the 6x5 teaching drill — players learn to read the defense and find the open player when their first option is taken away.
Entry
No sticks on defense. Players call "first look" or "second look" out loud before passing. Coach stops the drill and asks: "What's your second look?"
Full
3v2 live with sticks. Defenders can check. Offense must read first look, second look, or reset. Shot on cage = success.
Scoop and Shoot Player scoops a ground ball at midfield, carries up the alley, shoots on cage. 10 min
2 reps per player		 "Scoop through, cradle while running, shoot on the move." Combines ground-ball mechanics with transition offense — teaches players to turn a scoop into a shot in one motion.
Entry
No defender. Player scoops, carries in a straight line, shoots from 5 yards. Emphasize clean scoop and cradle control.
Full
Add a trailing defender who applies light pressure. Player must protect the stick (shoulder, shoulder, stick) while carrying.
3v3 Short Field Half-field 3v3. One constraint: must complete a pass before shooting. 8 min
4 possessions		 "Move the ball. If you lose it, get it back." Live play with a constraint teaches decision-making under pressure — the constraint forces ball movement instead of isolation.
Entry
One pass before shooting. Defense plays soft (no stick checks, only body positioning). Emphasize passing to the open player.
Full
Two passes before shooting. Defense can check. If defense gets the ball, they clear it over midfield to score (teaches transition urgency).
COACHING NOTES
This Week's Priorities
  • Install the second-look read: if first look is covered, swing the ball and look opposite. Teach this verbally before every 3v2 rep.
  • Protect counter-attack speed from Week 1 — no new counter concepts this week. Keep the goalie-to-wing outlet clean.
  • Ground-ball fundamentals: butt down, top hand down, scoop through it. Every player gets 8+ reps across both sessions.
  • Evaluate goalie candidates in both sessions — by end of Session B, name your starter for May 3.
  • Script a substitution rotation for the first game — write down who goes in when, tape it to the bench.
Evaluation Notes
  • Track which players can verbalize the second look during 3v2 reps — target 60% of the roster by end of Session B.
  • Log ground-ball scoops by name — who's winning contested GBs, who's getting beat to the ball.
  • Watch goalie candidates' hands — soft hands (catching like an egg) vs. hard hands (slapping at the ball).
  • Note which players are consistently late to substitutions — fix this before the first game.
  • Track shooting percentage (shots on cage / total shots) during live play — U10 target is 50%.
  • Identify your top 3 ground-ball players by name — they start every GB battle in the May 3 game.
Watch For (U10 Failure Modes)
  • Players bend at the waist instead of dropping the hips on ground balls — cue "butt down" every rep.
  • Ball-carrier cradles with the top hand on the plastic (choked up) — cue "hand down the shaft" every time you see it.
  • On 3v2, a player forces a covered pass instead of swinging it back — stop the drill and ask "What's your second look?"
  • Shooter locks onto the corner and telegraphs the shot — goalie reads it easily. Teach "look at the goal, not the corner" during Scoop and Shoot.
  • Players stop moving their feet after a scoop — the fish-hook is a scoop-plus-pivot, not scoop-and-stand.
  • Goalie candidate drops the stick early on high shots — cue "track the ball all the way into your stick" on every rep.
Week 3 Adjustment Trigger
If fewer than 60% of players can verbalize the second 6x5 look by end of Session B, OR if ground-ball win rate stays under 50% — repeat the Week 2 focal theme in Week 3 with reduced complexity. Add no new team concepts until second-look reads and ground-ball fundamentals are consistent in live 3v2 pressure.
