Week 1 Focus — From Your Intake
Pressing vs. dropping into zone — guessing when to switch. Counter attack underdeveloped, defaulting to front-court. Final two minutes of close games spinning out of control.
Same problem: your team is reacting to immediate pressure instead of reading game state and opponent patterns. Defenders commit to a stance too early. Transition lanes never establish because players aren't reading numbers or space. Close-game possessions devolve into individual decisions instead of systematic reads. This week builds the habit of reading before reacting — defensive recognition, transition lane discipline, and situational decision frameworks for the final two minutes.
End of Week Outcome
Defenders call the defensive stance before the opponent catches the ball. Counter attack establishes three lanes on every secured rebound. Players verbalize game state ("two up / one down / tied") before every possession in the final two minutes.

GK Track — Parallel Focus (0–40 min)  ·  Developing underclassman — positional stability and outlet discipline
All days: 200y warm-up (25 free / 25 backstroke / 25 eggbeater / 25 vertical backstroke) + 3 × 100y technical block (GP · Lateral · Glide · Quick Hands · Cherry Pickers · Gravity Drill).
Mon / Wed (Day 1 — Positional): 4 × 7-min blocks · 20s work / 10s rest: (1) GP hold, (2) GP + corner lunges, (3) Near-side glide series, (4) Cross-cage glide series.
Tue / Thu (Day 2 — Heavy Ball): 4 × 7-min blocks · heavy ball throughout: (1) Forehead holds, (2) Med ball two-handed lunges, (3) Lateral lunges (heavy ball), (4) 25 catapult throws.
Fri (Activation only): 100y warm-up + 1 × 100y technical set. Joins team for all special teams reps. Notes where every shot goes — high/low, near/far.
Communication priorities: Call "set" on every 5x6 rep (GK owns the shape). Louder when shape breaks, not quieter. Verbalize counter attack read: "outlet left / outlet right / hold".

Entry: Slow tempo, defenders call stance after catch. Offense holds ball 3 seconds before next pass. Full: Game tempo, defenders call stance before catch and execute immediately. Coaching Notes
Preseason Priorities
  • Teach defensive stance reads on Monday before live pressure — players must understand the "why" before the "when"
  • Establish counter attack lane discipline by Tuesday — no outlet passes until all three lanes are visible to GK
  • Name the primary 6x5 unit by Wednesday — consistency matters more than rotating all players
  • Identify game state leaders by Thursday — final-2-min possessions need a verbal anchor (senior or vocal junior)
  • Friday is rehearsal only — no new teaching, only speed and confidence
Scrimmage / Evaluation Notes
  • Track defensive stance calls by player name — who calls correctly, who guesses, who stays silent
  • Log counter attack lane establishment rate — track by secured rebound, not by possession
  • Count 6x5 first-look attempts vs. second-look attempts — are players forcing shots or reading coverage
  • Watch GK outlet timing — is GK holding too long or releasing before lanes establish
  • Note final-2-min game state verbalization — who speaks, who doesn't, does verbalization lead to better decisions
  • Track 5x6 shape breaks — which defenders chase, which hold position
Watch For
  • Defenders calling stance after the catch instead of before — this is reacting, not reading
  • Counter attack lanes collapsing to one side — GK outlet goes to the same lane every time
  • 6x5 units forcing first looks when coverage is tight — patience matters more than shot volume
  • GK staying silent during counter attack or 5x6 reps — GK must own communication
  • Players sprinting back to defense instead of recovery swimming — fatigue without purpose
  • Final-2-min possessions reverting to individual decisions — game state awareness disappears under pressure
Week 2 Adjustment Trigger
If 2 or more KPIs fall below target at Thursday or Friday scrimmage → repeat the Week 1 focal theme in Week 2 with reduced complexity. Add no new concepts until defensive stance calls, counter attack lane establishment, and game state verbalization are consistent under live pressure.
