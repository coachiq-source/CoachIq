Week 1 Focus — From Your Intake
Good water polo IQ, no counter attack structure. This team reads the game — they just default to front-court because no one has installed the counter attack framework yet.
You named it directly: counter attack is underdeveloped, you default to front-court. With good water polo IQ across the roster and 5 practices a week, this is the week to install it properly. The Advantage Rule gives every player — senior and freshman — a verbalized decision framework before they execute. The drive-and-replace motion feeds the counter when it doesn't convert, and your one strong shooter becomes a weapon in the front-court set rather than the only option. This week builds the system underneath the IQ that's already there.
End of Week 1 Outcome
Every player on the roster verbalizes the Advantage Rule before executing a counter attack rep — by Friday, without a prompt. The primary 6x5 unit attempts the quick within 5 seconds of the exclusion call on 6 of 8 live reps. The strong shooter is running the offense as a distributor in the front-court set, not just a shooter.
Swim Conditioning — Suggested Sets
All sets: head-up · no walls · 1:1 work:rest · two groups. Time every rep. With 2+ hour sessions, swim sets follow new concept introduction — never after fatigue.
Mon/Thu: 20 × 25y  |  Tue: 10 × 50y · :60 rest  |  Wed: 5 × 75y · 90s rest  |  Fri: 8 × 25y · full recovery  |  Week 2: +2 reps, tighten rest 5s.
Mixed Development — Two Tracks Per Drill
Experienced group (4 seniors + 2 junior field players): Full scheme from rep one. Decision-making under pressure. No walk-throughs.
Developing group (freshmen + sophomores): Entry-level progression first. Verbalize the decision before executing. Add defensive pressure only when the decision habit is clean.
Note on your junior GK: Promising development — run the full GK track every session. The starting GK is the underclassman; the junior GK shadows and takes designated reps.
GK Track — Parallel Focus (0–40 min)  ·  Two GKs — starter and developing junior run parallel tracks. Both log shot locations every rep.
All days: 200y warm-up + 3 × 100y technical block (GP · Lateral · Glide · Quick Hands · Cherry Pickers · Gravity Drill).
Mon/Wed/Fri (Day 1 — Positional): 4 × 7-min blocks · 20s/10s → GP hold → corner lunges → near-side glide → cross-cage glide.
Tue/Thu (Day 2 — Heavy Ball): 4 × 7-min blocks → forehead holds → med ball lunges → lateral lunges → 25 catapult throws.
Fri: Starter joins team for all special teams reps. Junior GK observes and calls shapes from outside.
Week 1 Tracking Priorities
Counter Attack Verbalization Rate
Track whether every player verbalizes the Advantage Rule before executing — by name, every session Mon through Fri
If same players not verbalizing by Thursday → mandatory verbal rep requirement added to their warm-up in Week 2
Counter Attack Conversion
Log every 2v1 and 3v2 attempt vs. conversion. First baseline — this team has never tracked this before
If conversion <40% → GK outlet timing is the problem; counter starts at the save, not after it
Drive-and-Replace Continuity
Count possessions where ball stays one side 3+ passes without a gap drive — stall rate by session
If stall rate >50% → players camping after drives; reset the replace rule before next live rep
6x5 Quick Attempt Rate
Track quick attempted within 5 seconds of exclusion call. Target: 6 of 8 live reps by Friday
If quick attempt rate <60% → exclusion drawer not recovering; drill recovery in isolation
Strong Shooter — Distribution vs. Isolation
Track whether the strong shooter is distributing from the front-court set or isolating as the only option. Log assists vs. solo shot attempts
If isolation rate >60% → other players are not driving to create options; drive-and-replace is breaking down
Situation Defense — Press vs. Zone Reads
Track how quickly you identify which defense to call at the start of each possession. Target: call made before ball crosses half
If calls are late → define the 2 triggers that determine press vs. zone before Week 2
Week 2 Adjustment Trigger
If 2 or more KPIs fall below target → repeat the counter attack framework in Week 2 with reduced complexity. Do not add new defensive concepts or the situation-defense read system until verbalization is at 100% and the counter attack is attempting with structure — not defaulting to front-court.
