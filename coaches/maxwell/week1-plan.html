Week 1 Focus — From Your Intake
Ground balls and clearing — same problem: we don't know where to move after we pick up the ball.
Ground balls become turnovers because players scoop and stand still. Clearing breaks down because players hold the ball and wait instead of finding an outlet. This week builds the instinct to scoop → turn → look — one motion, no pause.
End of Week 1 Outcome: Players scoop a ground ball and immediately turn to find a teammate. On clears, the goalie looks to a wing and the wing looks up-field. No more scooping and freezing.
Conditioning — U10 Age-Appropriate Warm-Up
U10 players do not run conditioning laps. Instead: every session begins with a dynamic warm-up (5 min) — high knees, butt kicks, shuffles, carioca, skips — followed immediately by partner passing (3 min) to wake up sticks. Total activation: 8 minutes. No fatigue before teaching.
Goalie Track — Building Confidence from Zero
Goalie position is unsettled. Every session includes a 5-min goalie introduction block (rotates all interested players). Content: Session 1 (Mon): Crease positioning — where to stand, how to hold the stick. Session 2 (Wed): Soft hands — catch tennis balls tossed from 5 feet, no sticks. Session 3 (Fri): Basic saves — coach rolls soft shots from 8 feet, goalie steps to the ball. Goal: identify one player who wants to try it in the scrimmage by Friday.
WEEK 1 TRACKING PRIORITIES
Scoop-Turn-Look Rate
Track by player name — count how many ground balls each player scoops and turns in one motion (no pause)
If same players freeze every session → Session 3: add a "scoop-pivot-outlet" constraint (player must find a teammate's stick within 2 seconds or coach blows whistle)
Clear Completion Rate
Goalie clears that reach a wing / attempted clears (target: 5/10 by Friday)
If under 3/10 by Session 2 → shorten the clearing distance (goalie to crease defender only, no long outlet yet)
Catch Success Rate
Partner passing — completed catches / tosses (measure across all players)
If under 60% by Session 2 → Session 3: every drill starts with a 2-min stationary passing refresh
Cradle Comfort
Count players who can cradle while jogging without dropping (target: 8/12 by Friday)
If under 6/12 by Session 2 → add a 3-min form cradling block before every small-sided drill
Outlet Identification
After scooping a ground ball, player verbally calls the teammate's name they're passing to (track instances per session)
If players don't call names → coach calls names for them in Session 3 ("Turn and find Emma")
Goalie Interest
Track which players try the goalie intro block and which one asks to try it again
If no one volunteers by Friday → coach assigns the most confident catcher to try it in the scrimmage
Week 2 Adjustment Trigger
If 2 or more KPIs fall below target at the Friday scrimmage → repeat the Week 1 focal theme in Week 2 with reduced complexity. Add no new concepts until scoop-turn-look and goalie-to-wing clears are consistent under live pressure.

FOCAL DRILLS — FULL DETAIL
Drill Setup Reps/Duration Coaching Cue Progression
Noodle Scooping Two lines, 5 yards apart. Coach rolls a ball between them. 10 reps per player "Butt down, top hand down, scoop through it."
Entry: Stationary — player steps to ball, scoops, and lifts stick to shoulder (no running).
Full: Fish-hook out — scoop and immediately turn away from pressure, outlet to next player in line.
What this teaches: Ground balls are picked up by getting low and scooping through the ball, not stabbing at it.
Scoop and Shoot Player scoops a rolled ball, fish-hooks to face the goal (empty net or coach in crease). 8 reps per player "Scoop, turn, look, shoot — one motion, no pause."
Entry: No defender — player scoops, pivots, takes 3 steps, shoots at an empty net from 10 feet.
Full: Add a trailing defender (light chase, no check) — player must turn and shoot before defender catches up.
What this teaches: After scooping a ground ball, the next move is to turn and look for the goal or a teammate — no standing still.
2v1 GB's Two offensive players, one defender. Coach rolls ball between the offensive players. 10 reps (5 per pair) "Whoever scoops, turn and find your partner."
Entry: Defender provides presence only (no stick, no check) — offensive player scoops and outlets to open teammate.
Full: Defender actively pursues the ball — scooper must shield with their body, turn away, and complete the outlet pass.
What this teaches: Ground balls become possessions when you immediately find an open teammate after scooping.
4v3 Box Goalie + 3 offensive players. Entry: no defense. Full: add 2 light defenders. 8 reps (entry), 6 reps (full) "Goalie calls the name, wing catches and looks up-field."
Entry: Walk-through, no defense — goalie looks to a wing (coach points which side), wing receives and carries forward 5 steps.
Full: Add 2 defenders (light pressure, no checks) — goalie outlets to wing, wing carries or passes middle.
What this teaches: Clearing is how your team moves the ball from defense to offense — goalie passes to a wing, wing looks to carry or pass forward.
1v1 GB's 1v1 box drill (10 × 10 yards), coach rolls ball between two players. 15 total reps "Scoop it, turn away, outlet to me."
Entry: Players start side-by-side (no body contact) — first to scoop wins, outlets to coach standing 5 feet away.
Full: Players start facing each other (light body contact allowed) — winner scoops, shields with hips, turns, and outlets.
What this teaches: Most ground balls are contested — you have to beat your opponent to the ball and protect it with your body after you scoop.
COACHING NOTES
This Week's Priorities
  • Session 1: Teach the scoop-through motion (butt down, top hand down). No stick checks, no live defense — just the motion.
  • Session 2: Add the turn — scoop and immediately pivot to find a teammate. Introduce light pressure (defender presence, no stick contact).
  • Session 3: Apply under live pressure in scrimmage. Constraint: every possession starts with a ground ball.
  • Goalie: Rotate interested players through 5-min intro blocks all week. Pick one to try in Friday's scrimmage.
  • First-year coach note: This team is in the fundamentals phase — every drill starts at the entry level before progressing. Do not move to the full version until the entry version is clean.
Scrimmage / Evaluation Notes
  • Watch for players who freeze after scooping (no turn, no outlet) — these are the focus players for Week 2.
  • Track how many ground balls each player scoops cleanly (target: 3+ per player during 20 min of scrimmage).
  • Count how many times the goalie completes an outlet pass to a wing (target: 3/10 by Friday).
  • Log which players call a teammate's name before passing (verbal outlet identification) — this is the habit we're building.
  • Note which player asks to try goalie again — they are your starting goalie candidate for Week 2.
  • Girls' lacrosse reminder: no body checks, no stick checks above the shoulder. Defenders stay stick-to-stick, not body-to-body.
Watch For
  • Players bend at the waist instead of dropping the hips on ground balls — cue "butt down" every rep.
  • Players scoop and stand still (no turn) — stop the drill and demonstrate the fish-hook pivot.
  • Goalie holds the ball too long before clearing — cue "call the name before you look" (voice first, eyes second).
  • Ball-carrier cradles with the top hand on the plastic (choked up) — cue "hand down the shaft, fingers near the throat."
  • Partner passing: players catch one-handed — cue "two hands, stick up in box position" (top hand at armpit, stick vertical).
  • Players drop the ball when running — this is normal for Week 1. Add form cradling (stationary rocking) before Week 2 drills.
