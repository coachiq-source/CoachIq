Week 2 Focus — From Your Intake
Ground balls and clearing — learning the game under live pressure
Team is still learning the rules and struggling with clearing. Same root cause: players don't yet know what success looks like in live situations. This week resolves it by teaching the rules inside the drills — every rep comes with a clear success picture and immediate feedback on what's legal, what's a turnover, and what's a foul.
End of Week Outcome: Every player can execute a clean ground-ball scoop under light pressure, identify their outlet on a clear, and name three rule violations they've seen (and corrected) in practice.
Conditioning — Field Movement & Recovery
Every session starts with 8–10 minutes of dynamic movement: High knees · Butt kicks · Carioca · A-skip · Backward running · Sprint to the cone (20 yards) · Recovery jog back. Two rounds. Players carry sticks to build comfort with movement + stick management. Purpose: Wake up the body, build cardiovascular base without fatigue overload. Middle school athletes can sustain 10-min dynamic blocks; longer aerobic work comes later in the season.
Goalie Track — Parallel Focus (First 25 min)  ·  Position unsettled — every player tries it this week
Goalie position is unsettled. This week's approach: rotate 3–4 interested players through the goalie role across the four sessions. Each candidate gets one full session in the cage; coach observes who tracks the ball, who talks, who wants to be there. Daily structure (0–25 min, parallel to field players' stick work): Warm-up clears (roll the ball out 10 yards, goalie runs out and scoops, pivots, throws to a cone target — 10 reps). Save progression: stationary catches from 10 yards (high, low, stick-side, off-stick) · moving laterally to catch · live 1v0 shots from 12 meters. Coaching cue: "See the ball into your stick — soft hands, the ball is an egg." Thursday decision: Name the starting goalie for Week 3 based on who showed the most composure and talked the loudest during the 1v0 shooting reps.
WEEK 2 TRACKING PRIORITIES
Ground Ball Win Rate
Scooped cleanly / contested GBs — track by session
If rate falls below 60% in Sessions 3 or 4 → back up to Noodle Scooping (entry drill) in Week 3, add two extra GB rounds per session
Clear Success Rate
Clears that reach offensive box / attempted clears
If below 70% by Thursday → simplify to 4v3 Box only in Week 3; don't add complexity until the outlet read is automatic
Rule Violations Called
Count of correct rule calls by players during live play (slashing, offsides, crease violations)
If players call fewer than 5 correct violations across all four sessions → add a 5-min "rules walkthrough" to every Week 3 session before live play
Outlet Recognition
Count of clears where the ball-carrier looked for AND found the outlet before the pass
If fewer than 50% of clears show a deliberate outlet look → slow down 4v3 Box tempo in Week 3, mandate a "freeze-and-point" before every outlet pass
Scoop Technique
Count of clean scoops (butt down, top hand down, through the ball) / total scoops
If below 70% → every session in Week 3 starts with 5 min of stationary Noodle Scooping before any contested work
Goalie Candidate Comfort
Which candidate talks loudest, tracks the ball best, wants to be there
If no clear standout by Thursday → run a fifth "goalie showcase" session Saturday morning (30 min, invite all candidates, pick one by end of weekend)
Week 3 Adjustment Trigger
If 2 or more KPIs fall below target by Thursday's live scrimmage → repeat the Week 2 focal theme (ground balls + clearing) in Week 3 with reduced complexity. Add no new concepts until clean scoops and clear outlet reads are consistent under live pressure.

SESSION BREAKDOWN
Session 1
Ground Balls + Stick Skills
Priority: Clean scoop technique on every rep — no bent-waist scoops
0–10
Arrival & Activation
Dynamic warm-up: high knees, butt kicks, carioca, A-skip, backward running, 20y sprint to cone + recovery jog. Two rounds. Players carry sticks throughout to build movement-plus-stick comfort.
Warm-Up
10–25
Noodle Scooping (Entry GB Drill)
Two lines. Coach rolls a ball out. Player scoops through it (butt down, top hand down), cradles once, fish-hooks out to the end of the line. Rule teaching moment: "If you touch the ball with your hand, it's a turnover — stick only." Run 3 rounds of 8 reps per player. Emphasize clean technique over speed.
Focal Noodle Scooping
25–45
Partner Passing + Cradle Ring
Pairs, 10 yards apart. Catch two hands, look at partner, pass. 20 completions, then switch to Cradle Ring: circle of 6 players, one ball, pass around the circle while moving (cradling). Rule teaching moment: "Pass hits the ground = turnover. Catch it clean every time."
Stick Skills Partner Passing Cradle Ring
45–60
1v1 GB's (Intermediate)
1v1 box drill. Loose ball between two players. First to scoop it wins, carries to the end line. Rule teaching moment: "Box out with your hips, then scoop away from pressure. Pushing with your hands = pushing foul." Run 10 reps (5 per player in each pair). Winner determined by clean scoop + carry across the line.
Focal 1v1 GB's
60–72
Small-Sided Live Play (3v3 Groundballs)
3v3 in a 20×20 box. Coach rolls a ball in. Whichever team scoops it gets possession, tries to complete 3 passes before shooting on a small goal. Constraint: Must scoop the ball cleanly before any pass — if the scoop is messy (bent waist, one-handed), possession resets. Play to 5 goals.
Live
72–75
Cool-Down & Message
Walk one lap of the field. Teach point: "Butt down, top hand down, scoop through it — that's the only way to win a ground ball." Player callout: Name one player who showed the cleanest technique and one who hustled hardest. Water.
Decision Identify your top 3 ground-ball players by name — they start every GB battle in Session 2.
Session 2
Clearing Basics
Priority: Every player identifies their outlet before they scoop the ball
0–10
Arrival & Activation
Same dynamic warm-up as Session 1. Add one element: after the sprint, player must pivot and point at an imaginary outlet (simulating a clear). Reinforces the "scoop, turn, find the outlet" sequence.
Warm-Up
10–25
4v3 Box (Entry Clear Drill)
Goalie candidate + 3 defenders (spread wide in your defensive half), no ride. Goalie outlets to a wing, wing swings middle, middle carries over midline. Rule teaching moment: "Cross midline in under 20 seconds or it's a stalling violation — keep the ball moving." Run 10 clears. Success = ball crosses midline cleanly 9 out of 10 times.
Focal 4v3 Box
25–45
Scoop and Shoot
Player starts at midfield, coach rolls a ball out. Player sprints to scoop, carries to the goal, shoots. Rule teaching moment: "You cannot enter the crease (the circle around the goal). Step on the line = goal disallowed." Goalie candidate is in the cage for live reps. 10 reps per player.
Shooting + GB Scoop and Shoot
45–60
5v4 House (Intermediate Clear)
4-player clear vs. 3-player hold-the-line ride, extra midfielder floats. Rule teaching moment: "If you're covered, swing it back through the goalie — that's not a turnover, it's smart." Run 8 clears. Success = 7 out of 8 clears reach the offensive box without a turnover.
Focal 5v4 House
60–72
Small-Sided Live Play (4v4 Clear to Offense)
4v4 half-field. Defense starts with the ball in their defensive box, must clear it to the offensive box, then play live until a goal or turnover. Constraint: Every clear must include a goalie outlet AND a look to the opposite wing before crossing midline — if either is skipped, possession resets. Play to 5 goals.
Live
72–75
Cool-Down & Message
Walk one lap. Teach point: "Goalie looks wing first; if wing is covered, swing it back. That's how you beat a ride." Player callout: Name one player who found their outlet fastest. Water.
Decision Pick the first-line clearing unit (4 players: goalie candidate + 3 field players) — they run every 5v4 House rep in Session 3.
Session 3
Ground Balls + Transition
0–10
Arrival & Activation
Dynamic warm-up, same structure. Add: after the sprint, player must scoop a stationary ball at the cone, cradle twice, and carry back to the line. Reinforces scoop-to-carry fluency.
Warm-Up
10–25
2v2 Canada GB's (Full GB Drill)
2v2 GB to outlet to fast break. Coach rolls a ball between two pairs. Whichever pair scoops it outlets to a waiting midfielder, then all four sprint upfield for a 4v3 or 3v3 live finish. Rule teaching moment: "Offsides = too many players in the offensive or defensive box. Count your teammates before you cross midline." Run 10 reps. Success = transition finishes in ≤4 seconds, 5 out of 10 result in a shot or goal.
Focal 2v2 Canada GB's
25–45
3v2 West Genny (Unsettled Offense)
3 offensive players, 2 defenders. Offense starts at midfield with the ball, tries to score before the defense can set. Rule teaching moment: "Slashing = stick checks below the waist or on the hands. Keep your checks stick-to-stick only." Run 12 reps (6 per group). Defense rotates in after every 3 reps.
Transition 3v2 West Genny
45–60
4 Corners Pass + Motion Intro
4 players in a box (top-wing-wing-X). Pass around the horn: catch two hands, look at the goal, then pass. 10 consecutive completions = success. Then introduce one cut: passer cuts through after the pass, receiver fills the open spot. Rule teaching moment: "Moving pick = you set a screen while you're moving. That's a foul. Stop your feet before you screen."
Offense Foundation 4 Corners Pass
60–72
Small-Sided Live Play (5v5 GBs to Transition)
5v5 half-field. Every possession starts with a coach rolling a ball into the middle. Whichever team scoops it gets possession, pushes transition to the goal. Constraint: Offense must shoot within 10 seconds of the scoop or possession turns over — forces quick decision-making. Play to 7 goals.
Live
72–75
Cool-Down & Message
Walk one lap. Teach point: "Scoop, turn, find the outlet in one motion — don't scoop and stand there." Player callout: Name one player who made the best outlet pass under pressure. Water.
Decision Name the starting five for Session 4's full scrimmage (3 middies, 2 attack, or 2 middies / 3 attack depending on your emerging system) — they get first reps in every live block.
Session 4
Live Scrimmage + Rules
0–10
Arrival & Activation
Dynamic warm-up, same structure. End with two 40-yard sprints at game speed to prep for the scrimmage block.
Warm-Up
10–25
Ground Ball Gauntlet
All 19 players. Coach scatters 10 balls across a 30×30 box. On the whistle, everyone sprints in to scoop one. Once you have it, carry it out and set it down at the sideline. First 10 players to secure a ball and get it to the sideline win. Rule teaching moment: "Pushing from behind = pushing foul. Use your body to box out, not your hands." Run 3 rounds.
Focal
25–40
Clearing Review (4v3 + 5v4 Mixed Reps)
Run 5 reps of 4v3 Box, then 5 reps of 5v4 House. Emphasize goalie outlet speed and wing recognition. Rule teaching moment: "20-second count starts the moment the goalie has possession. If you don't cross midline in time, it's a stalling turnover."
Focal
40–70
Full Scrimmage (10v9 with Subs)
Two teams (10 players per side if the goalie candidate is set; otherwise rotate goalie every 5 min). Running clock, 15-min halves. Constraint: Players must call out rule violations they see — slashing, offsides, crease violations, pushing. Every correct call earns the team 1 bonus point at the end of the scrimmage. Coach tracks correct vs. incorrect calls. Rule teaching throughout: Stop play after every major violation (offsides, slashing, crease) and explain it to the whole group before restarting. Goal: players finish the scrimmage knowing 5+ rule violations by name.
Live
70–75
Cool-Down & Message
Huddle at midfield. Teach point: "You just played your first real scrimmage. You won ground balls, you made clears, you called out fouls. That's lacrosse." Player callout: Name the player who called the most correct rule violations and the player who showed the best sportsmanship. Water.
Decision Name the starting goalie for Week 3 — the candidate who showed the most composure, talked loudest, and wanted to be there during the scrimmage.
FOCAL DRILLS — FULL DETAIL
Drill Setup Reps/Duration Coaching Cue What This Teaches Progression
Noodle Scooping Two lines, coach rolls a ball out, player scoops through, fish-hook out 3 rounds · 8 reps per player · ~12 min "Butt down, top hand down, scoop through it." Ground balls are the foundation of possession — every lacrosse game is won or lost on the ratio of GBs scooped cleanly.
Entry Level
Stationary ball, no pressure. Player focuses only on form: butt down, top hand low on the shaft, scoop through the ball, cradle once.
Full Version
Rolling ball, light pressure from a trailing defender. Player must scoop + fish-hook away from pressure in one motion.
1v1 GB's 1v1 box drill, loose ball between two players 10 reps total · 5 per player in each pair · ~12 min "Box out with your hips, then scoop away from pressure." In a contested ground ball, body position wins before stick position — the player who seals their opponent away from the ball wins the scoop.
Entry Level
Ball is stationary. Players start 5 yards apart, sprint to the ball. Focus: use body to box out, not hands.
Full Version
Ball is rolling. Players start closer (3 yards), must react to ball movement and box out while tracking the roll.
4v3 Box Goalie + 3 defenders, no ride. Outlet to wing, swing middle, carry over midline 10 clears · ~12 min "Goalie looks wing first; defenders spread wide." Clearing is how a stopped shot or saved shot becomes offense — every turnover prevented in the defensive half is worth more than a made shot.
Entry Level
No ride at all. Defenders spread to the sidelines; goalie has 5+ seconds to find the outlet. Focus: clean catch, pivot, pass.
Full Version
Add a single token rider who pressures the goalie (no stick checks, just presence). Goalie must outlet under light time pressure.
5v4 House 4-player clear vs. 3-player hold-the-line ride, extra midfielder floats 8 clears · ~12 min "If you're covered, swing it back through the goalie." A clear is not a turnover if the ball goes backward — swinging the ball to safety is smarter than forcing a covered pass.
Entry Level
Ride stays passive at midfield (hold the line only, no chasing). Clearing team practices swinging the ball side-to-side until a lane opens.
Full Version
Ride pressures up to the restraining box. Clearing team must recognize when to swing back to the goalie vs. when to push through.
2v2 Canada GB's 2v2 GB to outlet to fast break; coach rolls ball between two pairs 10 reps · ~12 min "Scoop, turn, find the outlet in one motion." The team that converts ground balls into transition scores more than the team with the better settled offense — speed after the scoop is everything.
Entry Level
Ball starts stationary. Winning pair scoops, outlets to a stationary midfielder, then jogs upfield to finish 3v2.
Full Version
Ball is rolling. Winning pair must scoop on the move, outlet to a moving midfielder, and sprint upfield for a live 4v3 finish in ≤4 seconds.
Partner Passing Pairs, 10 yards apart, catch-look-pass 20 completions per pair · ~8 min "Catch two hands, look at your partner, then pass." Passing is the foundation of every offensive possession — a dropped pass is a turnover, and turnovers lose games.
Entry Level
Stationary passing, focus on two-handed catches and stepping into the pass. No defensive pressure.
Full Version
Passing while moving (partners shuffle side-to-side or jog in a straight line). Focus: lead the receiver, catch on the move.
COACHING NOTES
This Week's Priorities
  • Teach ground-ball technique before adding pressure. Session 1 is pure form work (Noodle Scooping); contested work comes in Session 3 after the technique is clean.
  • Introduce rules inside every drill. Don't wait for a scrimmage to explain slashing, offsides, crease violations — call them out the moment they happen in small-sided work.
  • Clearing starts with the goalie's outlet read. Every 4v3 and 5v4 rep begins with the goalie identifying the open wing before the pass — this is the decision that unlocks the whole clear.
  • Settle the goalie position by Thursday. Rotate 3–4 candidates through the role this week; pick the one who tracks the ball best, talks loudest, and wants to be there.
  • Session 4 is your first live scrimmage — make it a teaching scrimmage. Stop play after every major rule violation, explain it to the group, restart. Goal: players finish knowing 5+ violations by name.
Evaluation Notes
  • Ground-ball technique: Count how many players scoop with butt-down-top-hand-down vs. bent-waist-one-handed. Target: 70%+ clean technique by Session 3.
  • Outlet recognition on clears: Count how many clears show a deliberate goalie-to-wing look before the pass. If fewer than 50%, slow down the tempo and mandate a "freeze-and-point" before every outlet.
  • Rule calls during live play: Track correct vs. incorrect rule calls by players in Session 4's scrimmage. Correct calls = they're learning; incorrect calls = they're trying (still good).
  • Goalie candidate comparison: Who tracks the ball into their stick? Who talks (calls "ball!" or "I have it!")? Who wants to be there (body language, energy)? Pick the one who scores highest on all three.
  • Clearing success rate: 70%+ by Session 4 is the target. If below, back up to 4v3 Box only in Week 3 — don't add complexity until the outlet read is automatic.
  • Ground-ball win rate in live play: 60%+ by Session 4 is the baseline. If below, add 5 min of stationary Noodle Scooping to the start of every Week 3 session.
Watch For (Beginner-Team Failure Modes)
  • Players bend at the waist instead of dropping the hips on GBs. Cue "butt down" on every rep — this is the #1 beginner GB mistake.
  • Goalie clears too late — outlets should be available by the time the save is made. Defenders must spread to the sidelines immediately after a shot; goalie looks wing before the ball is even in their stick.
  • Ball-carrier carries with the top hand on the plastic (choked up). Cue "hand down the shaft" every rep — choking up kills passing power and makes the stick easy to check.
  • On clears, a wing stands still waiting for the ball instead of showing the goalie a target. Wings must "show hands" (stick up, visible, moving to an open space) — the goalie can't outlet to a ghost.
  • Players don't know when to call a rule violation vs. when to stay quiet. In Week 2, err on the side of over-calling during drills — you can tighten the whistle in Week 3 once they know the rules.
  • Scrimmage turns into chaos because no one knows the offsides rule. Stop play the first 3 times it happens, walk through the restraining-box boundaries, restart. By the 4th time, they'll get it.
Week 3 Adjustment Trigger
If 2 or more KPIs (ground-ball win rate, clear success rate, scoop technique, outlet recognition) fall below target by Session 4's scrimmage → repeat the Week 2 focal theme (ground balls + clearing basics) in Week 3 with reduced complexity. Add no new concepts until clean scoops and clear outlet reads are consistent under live pressure. Specifically: back up to Noodle Scooping (entry) and 4v3 Box (entry) only; do not introduce 2v2 Canada GB's or 5v4 House until the fundamentals are automatic.
