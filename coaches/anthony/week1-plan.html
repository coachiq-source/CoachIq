Week 1 Focus — From Your Intake
Passing and moving without the ball — players are stationary after the pass.
The team struggles under pressure because players pass and freeze, expecting the passer to solve the next problem. Same root cause: no one moves after the pass, so every possession is one pass followed by a trap. This week resolves it by teaching pass-and-cut as a single motion — every pass creates an open player through movement, not through waiting.
End of Week 1 Outcome: Every player can complete a pass-and-cut sequence (pass → cut to the ball or basket → receive a return pass) in live 3v3 play without freezing after the first pass. Players call "move!" when they see a teammate standing still.
Conditioning — Built Into Every Block
No dedicated conditioning block. Every drill is run at game speed with short rest intervals. Players condition through repetition, not through separate fitness work. Focus: quick feet, sharp cuts, hands ready. Every water break is earned after a completed drill block — movement never stops during teaching.
WEEK 1 TRACKING PRIORITIES
Pass-and-Move Compliance
Count passes where the passer cuts immediately vs. passes where the passer stands still
If fewer than 5 of 10 passes include a cut by end of Session 2 → dedicate an extra 5-min block to stationary pass-and-cut (no defense) in Session 3 before live play
Shot Quality
Shots in the paint + open looks / total shots — track by player name
If more than half of all shots are forced/contested outside → Session 3 focuses exclusively on inside finishing (lay-up drills, 2v2 inside only)
Boxing Out Compliance
Count of defensive rebounds where all five defenders found a body vs. total defensive rebound opportunities
If boxing-out rate is below 50% in Session 2 live play → Session 3 starts with a 7-min Find a Body drill before any other teaching
Turnover Rate Under Pressure
Turnovers per possession when defense applies token pressure (no press, just active hands)
If turnover rate is above 30% in Session 2 → Session 3 teaches one simple pass-out option (skip pass to the weak side) and practices it 20 reps before scrimmage
Free-Throw Percentage (Under Fatigue)
FTs made / FTs attempted in Block 6 of each session — track by player
If team FT% is below 40% after Session 2 → every player shoots 5 bonus FTs at the start of Session 3 (fresh, no fatigue) to isolate form issues
Defensive Talk
Count of "help!" / "screen!" / "ball!" calls per defensive possession in live play
If talk rate is under 1 call per possession by Session 3 → add a 3-min "talk or sit" drill: defense plays 4v4, any possession with zero calls = that defender sits one rep
Week 2 Adjustment Trigger
If 2 or more KPIs fall below target at the first game (May 30) → repeat the Week 1 focal theme in Week 2 with reduced complexity. Add no new concepts until pass-and-cut compliance, shot quality, and boxing-out compliance are consistent under live pressure.

SESSION BREAKDOWN
Session 1 (May 22)
Ball Handling & Movement
Priority: Every player learns that a pass is the start of movement, not the end
0–6
Arrival & Activation
Dynamic warm-up: high knees, butt kicks, side shuffle, backpedal (2 min). Partner passing: chest pass, bounce pass, overhead pass — 10 reps each, 6 feet apart. Focus: catch two hands, look at your partner's chest before you pass.
Warm-Up
6–18
Triple Threat & Stationary Pound
Each player with a ball. Triple threat: feet shoulder-width, ball at the hip, knees bent. Hold for 10 seconds, reset. 3 reps. Stationary pound: waist-high dribble, eyes up, fingertips. 30 seconds right hand, 30 seconds left hand. Cue: "Eyes up, fingertips, push the ball through the floor." What this teaches: A player who can keep their head up while handling the ball can pass and read the defense — eyes-down dribblers cannot run an offense.
Focal Skill Triple Threat 1v0 Stationary Pound
18–30
Pass and Cut — No Defense (2v0)
Two players, one ball. Player 1 passes to Player 2, then immediately cuts toward Player 2 (not away). Player 2 passes back to Player 1 on the cut. Reset. 10 reps each. Cue: "Pass and move — don't watch your pass." What this teaches: Every pass creates an open player through movement. Passers who stand still give the defense an extra second to recover.
Focal Movement Give and Go
30–40
3-Man Weave (Entry Version)
Three players weave across half-court with one ball. Pass and follow your pass — the passer runs behind the receiver. Finish with a lay-up. Walk-through first 3 reps; full speed next 5 reps. Cue: "Pass and follow your pass; fill the open lane." What this teaches: The weave reinforces pass-and-move as a single motion — no one ever stands still when the ball is live.
Team Movement 3-Man Weave
40–55
3v3 Half-Court — Constraint: 3 Passes Before Any Shot
Three possessions each side, switch. Offense must complete 3 passes before shooting. Defense: token pressure (active hands, no steals). Constraint: If offense shoots before 3 passes, possession turns over immediately. What to watch: Does the passer cut after the first pass, or do they stand and watch?
Live Play
55–60
Free Throws + Cool-Down
Every player shoots 5 free throws. Cue: "Feet square, balance, follow through — wave goodbye." Track makes/misses by player name. One teach point: pass-and-cut is a single motion, not two separate actions. One player callout: name one player who cut immediately after every pass. Water.

Session 2 (May 24)
Defense & Rebounding
Priority: Every defensive rebound starts with finding a body — boards are won before the shot goes up
0–6
Arrival & Activation
Dynamic warm-up: defensive slides across the lane (forward, backward, side-to-side). Partner passing: 10 chest passes, 10 bounce passes. Focus: hands ready, eyes on the ball.
Warm-Up
6–18
Find a Body — Intro to Boxing Out
Coach shoots from the free-throw line. Five defenders under the rim. On the shot, every defender turns and finds a player (imaginary offense for now) and holds position (butt out, arms wide) for 2 seconds, then goes for the ball. 10 reps. Cue: "Find a body, butt to the gut, hold for two seconds." What this teaches: Boxing out is the most undercoached fundamental at the youth level. A team that secures 70%+ of available defensive rebounds is in every game, regardless of talent gap.
Rebounding Find a Body
18–30
No Hands Defense (1v1 Half-Court)
Defender's hands behind their back. Offense dribbles from the wing to the opposite wing. Defender must stay in front using only feet. 5 reps each, then switch. Cue: "Move your feet, not your hands." What this teaches: Defense is footwork and talking before it is anything else — hands lead to fouls; feet and voice lead to stops.
Defense No Hands Defense
30–40
2-on-2 Box Out (Live Rebounding)
Two offense, two defense in the paint. Coach shoots. Offense crashes for the offensive board; defense boxes out. Cue: "Hit them first, then go get the ball." Defense scores 1 point per secured rebound; offense scores 1 point per offensive board. First to 5 wins. 3 games. What this teaches: Rebounding is contact before ball — players who go straight for the ball lose to players who seal their opponent first.
Rebounding 2-on-2 Box Out
40–55
4v4 Half-Court — Constraint: Box Out Every Shot
Four possessions each side, switch. Defense must box out on every shot — if a defender doesn't find a body before going for the ball, that's an automatic offensive board (even if defense gets it). Constraint: Boxing out is mandatory; turnovers from missed box-outs teach the priority. What to watch: Are defenders turning and sealing, or are they ball-watching and reaching?
Live Play
55–60

Every player shoots 5 free throws under fatigue. Track makes/misses by player name. One teach point: boxing out wins games — boards are the foundation of every possession. One player callout: name one player who sealed their opponent on every rebound attempt. Water.

COACHING NOTES
This Week's Priorities
  • Session 1: Teach pass-and-cut as a single motion, not two separate actions. Every player must demonstrate it once in 3v3 before the session ends.
  • Session 2: Introduce boxing out as first contact, then ball. Rebounding is won before the shot goes up, not after.
  • Pre-game walk-through: Confidence-building only. No new teaching, no corrections. Let them play and watch who leads.
  • First game teaching sequence: One timeout focuses on pass-and-move; one timeout focuses on boxing out. Do not introduce any new concepts during the game.
  • Terminology scaffolding: This is a first-year coach with a roster where only two players have played before. Every term must be defined the first time it appears. "Triple threat" = ready position. "Boxing out" = turning and sealing your player away from the rim. "Help defense" = leaving your own player to stop a dribbler who beat their primary defender.
Evaluation Notes
  • Pass-and-cut compliance: Track by player name — who cuts immediately after the pass, and who stands still? The ones who stand still are your teaching targets for Session 2.
  • Ball-handler identification: In Session 1 live play, watch who can dribble with their head up under token pressure. Those are your top 3 ball-handlers.
  • Boxing-out technique: In Session 2, watch who turns and seals vs. who ball-watches and reaches. Turners are your rebounding leaders; watchers need individual coaching.
  • Free-throw form: Track every player's FT% across all three sessions. Any player under 30% needs individual form work (feet square, elbow in, follow through).
  • Defensive talk: In every live-play block, count the number of "help!" / "screen!" / "ball!" calls per possession. Silent teams lose to talking teams — make talk a non-negotiable.
  • Point-guard decision: By the end of Session 2, you must have picked the player who will bring the ball up the court in the first game. The decision is based on who handles pressure best, not who scores most.
Watch For
  • Players bring the ball below the waist on every catch — gets stripped immediately. Cue "chin it" on every catch. Ball stays at the hip (triple threat) or above the shoulders (overhead pass protection).
  • Defenders reach with their hands instead of moving their feet — fouls pile up. Cue "feet, not hands" every defensive rep. Hands go up when you're in position, not before.
  • On every shot, two or three players ball-watch instead of finding a body to box out. This is the #1 rebounding failure mode at MS level. Fix it by making boxing-out compliance mandatory (Session 2 constraint).
  • Pass-and-stand on offense — passer doesn't cut after the pass. Cue "pass and move" on every rep. If a player passes and stands still, stop the drill and have them do it again.
  • Free throws shot fresh, not under fatigue — Block 6 fixes this; do not skip it. Every session ends with FTs under fatigue. This is non-negotiable. MS players who can hit FTs tired will hit them fresh in games.
  • Best player (if there is one) carries the ball every possession; teammates stop moving. Set a passing-touch quota in the constraint (e.g. "Must complete 3 passes before any shot"). Force ball movement.
Week 2 Adjustment Trigger

Add no new concepts until pass-and-cut compliance, shot quality, and boxing-out compliance are consistent under live pressure. Week 2 becomes a refinement week, not a progression week.
