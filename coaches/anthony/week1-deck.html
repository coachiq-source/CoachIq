Week 1 Court Sheet
Week 1 Focus
Passing and moving without the ball — players are stationary after the pass
End of Week 1: Every player can complete a pass-and-cut sequence in live 3v3 play without freezing after the first pass. Players call "move!" when they see a teammate standing still.
First Game
May 30
Primary Target
Player development
Court
Full court
Conditioning — Built Into Every Block
No dedicated conditioning block. Every drill is run at game speed with short rest intervals. Players condition through repetition, not through separate fitness work. Focus: quick feet, sharp cuts, hands ready.
Session 1 · May 22
Ball Handling & Movement
0–6: Warm-up + partner passing
6–18: Triple threat + stationary pound (eyes up, fingertips)
18–30: Pass and cut — no defense (2v0)
30–40: 3-man weave
40–55: 3v3 half-court (constraint: 3 passes before shot)
55–60: Free throws + cool-down
Session 2 · May 24
Defense & Rebounding
0–6: Warm-up (defensive slides)
6–18: Find a body (boxing-out intro)
18–30: No hands defense (1v1)
30–40: 2-on-2 box out (live rebounding)
40–55: 4v4 half-court (constraint: box out every shot)
55–60: Free throws + cool-down
Pre-Game · May 29
Game Prep
0–6: Light warm-up (no fatigue)
6–18: Lay-up lines + form shooting
18–30: Pass-and-cut review (3v0)
30–42: 5v5 scrimmage (no coaching)
42–55: Free throws + cool-down
Focal Drills — Quick Reference
Drill Day Key Cue
Triple Threat 1v0 Session 1 "Triple threat — ball at the hip, feet shoulder-width."
Stationary Pound Session 1 "Eyes up, fingertips, push the ball through the floor."
Pass and Cut Session 1 "Pass and move — don't watch your pass."
Find a Body Session 2 "Find a body, butt to the gut, hold for two seconds."
No Hands Defense Session 2 "Move your feet, not your hands."
2-on-2 Box Out Session 2 "Hit them first, then go get the ball."
Tracking Priorities
Pass-and-move: Count passes where passer cuts vs. stands still
Shot quality: Shots in paint + open looks / total shots
Boxing out: Defenders who find a body / total rebounds
Free throws: FT% under fatigue (Block 6)
Coach Decisions
Session 1: Identify top 3 ball-handlers by name
Session 2: Pick starting five + first sub group ("high-five")
Pre-game: Name the point guard by jersey number
Watch For
Players bring ball below waist on catch → cue "chin it"
Defenders reach with hands, not feet → cue "feet, not hands"
Ball-watching on rebounds instead of boxing out
Pass-and-stand on offense — passer doesn't cut
