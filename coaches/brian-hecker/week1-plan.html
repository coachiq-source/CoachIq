Week 1 Focus — From Your Intake
Speed is the weapon. Inexperience is the liability. The counter attack only works if developing players make fast decisions on the outlet.
Your elite GK anchors everything — she initiates every counter attack, every transition, every possession reset. But the primary counter (1v0/2v1) requires the receiving players to read and decide before they arrive. This week installs the counter attack decision framework for players new to varsity minutes, while establishing your rising junior at center-forward in the M-drop structure. Mixed development means two tracks on every drill — experienced players run full scheme, developing players build the decision habit first.
End of Week 1 Outcome
Every player on the roster verbalizes the Advantage Rule before executing a counter attack rep. The rising junior runs all center-forward reps in the M-drop structure by Friday. The 4-2 unit attempts the quick within 5 seconds of the exclusion call on 6 of 8 live reps.
Swim Conditioning — Suggested Sets
All sets: head-up · no walls · 1:1 work:rest · two groups. Time every rep. Note: swimmers on roster — hold the same standard, head-up only, no flip turns. The training adaptation is different from swimming and that's intentional.
Mon/Thu: 16 × 25y  |  Tue: 8 × 50y · :60 rest  |  Wed: 4 × 75y · 90s rest  |  Week 2: +2 reps, tighten rest 5s.
Mixed Development — Two Tracks Per Drill
Experienced group (3 seniors + returning starters): Full scheme execution. No walk-throughs. Decision-making under pressure from rep one.
Developing group (players new to varsity minutes): Entry-level progression first. Verbalize the decision before executing. Add defensive pressure only when the decision habit is clean.
Rule: Never run both groups at the same pace. Developing players who rush to keep up with the experienced group will ingrain bad habits. Separate the reps.
GK Track — Parallel Focus (0–30 min)  ·  Elite GK drives every counter attack initiation and M-drop communication.
All days: 200y warm-up + 3 × 100y technical block (GP · Lateral · Glide · Quick Hands · Cherry Pickers · Gravity Drill).
Mon/Thu (Day 1 — Positional): 4 × 7-min blocks · 20s/10s → GP hold → corner lunges → near-side glide → cross-cage glide.
Tue/Wed (Day 2 — Heavy Ball): 4 × 7-min blocks → forehead holds → med ball lunges → 25 catapult throws.
Fri: Activation only. Joins all special teams reps. She calls M-drop shape before any field player adjusts. Notes every shot location.
Week 1 Tracking Priorities
Counter Attack Decision Speed
Track whether developing players verbalize the Advantage Rule before executing — by player name, every session
If same players are not verbalizing by Thursday → add verbal rep requirement to warm-up in Week 2
Primary Counter Conversion
Log every 1v0 and 2v1 attempt vs. conversion. Week 1 baseline — this is the primary scoring source
If conversion <50% → GK outlet timing; the counter starts at the save, not after it
Center-Forward Entry — Rising Junior
Count entry passes completed vs. denied in M-drop reps. Track her positioning read before the entry attempt
If entry completion <40% → slow down to entry-level progression; positioning before entry attempt
6x5 Quick Attempt Rate
Track whether the 4-2 unit attempts the quick within 5 seconds of the exclusion call. Log attempts vs. conversions
If quick attempt rate <60% → exclusion drawer recovery is the problem; drill in isolation
M-Drop Shape Discipline
Count shape breaks per 5x6 rep — who is out of position and when. GK calls each break before field players adjust
If >3 breaks per 8 reps → walk through the M-drop alignment before live reps each session
Perimeter Decision-Making
Track hesitation moments — passes held 2+ seconds under pressure, shot opportunities passed up. Log by player
If same players hesitating repeatedly → assign them specific decision triggers in Week 2
Week 2 Adjustment Trigger
If 2 or more KPIs fall below target → repeat Week 1 focal theme with reduced complexity in Week 2. Do not add new offensive concepts until counter attack decision verbalization is consistent across the full roster and the rising junior is clean in her center-forward positioning under the M-drop.

Coaching Notes — Week 1
Preseason Priorities
  • Counter attack decision verbalization — every player, every rep, by Thursday. Non-negotiable.
  • Rising junior at center-forward — running all CF reps by Friday, with or without a senior alongside
  • Primary 6x5 unit locked by Wednesday — roles fixed for Beast of the East
  • Mixed development means separate pacing — never let developing players rush to keep up with experienced players
  • Note for swimmers on roster — head-up conditioning is intentionally different. The adaptation is sport-specific.
    • Scrimmage Evaluation
  • Evaluation only — no coaching to the result in live reps
  • Log counter attack attempts vs. conversions Monday and Thursday — is it improving?
  • Log verbalization rate by player — same names not verbalizing drives Week 2 individual assignments
  • Track M-drop shape breaks — GK calls vs. field player calls, every rep
  • Note perimeter hesitation moments by player — these become Week 2 decision trigger assignments
  • GK tracks every shot location — high/low, near/far, every rep, every session
    • Watch For
  • Developing players executing without verbalizing — the habit must come before the speed
  • Experienced players skipping the verbalization because they "already know it" — they set the standard
  • Rising junior rushing into entry attempts before her position is established
  • GK going quiet during M-drop shape breaks — she must call louder when it breaks, not quieter
  • Perimeter players holding the ball 2+ seconds under pressure — that is the hesitation pattern to eliminate
  • Counter attack players not hitting the named lane first — freelancing defeats the GK outlet system
