Week 1 Focus — From Your Intake
Players struggling with ground balls and freezing when the game is close
Coach is in their first season, team is mostly beginners, and they freeze when losing late. Same problem: we haven't built the habits that let players trust their training under pressure. This week installs the foundation — ground-ball technique, box position for catching, and simple dodging footwork — so players have muscle-memory answers when the chaos hits.
End of Week 1 Outcome
Players scoop ground balls with their feet moving and their butt down, catch passes in box position without bobbling, and know one reliable dodge they can run when their first look is taken away.
Conditioning — Age-Appropriate Endurance
U12 attention cap is ~7 min per block. Every block is 5–7 min, followed by a water break. No fitness-for-fitness-sake drills — conditioning happens inside the skill work. Ground-ball battles and 1v1 drills are the cardio. If players aren't breathing hard after Noodle Scooping, the intensity is too low.
Goalie Track — Building Confidence (Parallel to Team)
Developing goalie — still building confidence. Goalie works on catching mechanics and crease positioning while the team runs stick-skill blocks. Coach feeds soft shots from 8–10 yards, focusing on tracking the ball into the pocket. No game-speed shooting until the goalie can catch 8/10 clean. Goalie joins the team for all live scrimmage reps and learns to call "clear" on every save.
WEEK 1 TRACKING PRIORITIES
Ground Ball Scoop Rate
Track clean scoops (butt down, feet moving, no bobble) vs. total attempts
If scoop rate is under 50% at the end of practice → repeat Noodle Scooping in the Week 2 warm-up before progressing to 1v1 GB's
Box-Position Catches
Count catches where the stick is vertical and top hand is at the armpit
If players drop more than 3 passes in Partner Passing → shorten the distance and slow the pace; catching under control beats volume
1v1 Dodge Completion
Track dodges that create separation (defender trailing by 2+ steps)
If dodge completion is under 40% → the fake isn't selling; add a hesitation step before the go
Goalie Save Percentage
Saves / shots faced in live scrimmage (feed shots from 10+ yards only)
If save rate is under 30% → goalie is guessing, not tracking; back up to soft-toss catching before live shots
Transition Possessions
Count possessions that reach the offensive box in under 10 sec after a GB
If transition rate is under 50% → players are holding the ball too long; cue "scoop, turn, find the outlet"
Coach Calm Under Pressure
Self-rate: did you stay calm and give one clear cue during the scrimmage constraint?
If you froze or over-coached → write down your one cue before the next scrimmage and commit to saying only that
Week 2 Adjustment Trigger
If 2 or more KPIs fall below target at the end of practice → repeat the Week 1 focal theme in Week 2 with reduced complexity. Add no new concepts until ground-ball scooping, box-position catching, and 1v1 dodge footwork are consistent under live pressure.

SESSION BREAKDOWN
Week 1 Practice
Fundamentals
Priority: Every player leaves knowing how to scoop a ground ball with their feet moving and their butt down
0–8
Arrival & Dynamic Warm-Up
Players arrive, grab sticks, and form two lines. Dynamic warm-up: high knees, butt kicks, side shuffle, carioca (15 yards each). End with partner passing (10 yards apart, 10 completions each). Coach introduces the one focal point: "Today is about ground balls — get low, scoop through, feet never stop."
Warm-Up
8–18
Noodle Scooping
Two lines, coach rolls a ball out. Player scoops through the ball (butt down, top hand down, feet moving), fish-hooks away from the line, and passes back to the coach. 8 reps per player. Coaching cue: "Butt down, top hand down, scoop through it." Success metric: 8/10 clean scoops, feet never stop.
Focal Noodle Scooping
Decision Identify your top 3 ground-ball players by name — they start every GB battle in Week 2.
18–28
Partner Passing (Box Position)
Players pair up, 10 yards apart. Focus: catching in box position (stick vertical, top hand at the armpit, eyes on the ball). 20 completions per pair. Coaching cue: "Catch two hands, stick up, look it in." If players drop more than 2 passes, they're rushing — slow the pace and shorten the distance to 7 yards.
Stick Skill Partner Passing
28–42
1v1 Dodging (Fake & Go)
Two lines: offense and defense. Offensive player starts 5 yards from the cone. Fake one way (sell it with a hard step and shoulder dip), then dodge the other way. Defender plays 50% pressure (trails, doesn't chase). 6 reps per player. Coaching cue: "Fake hard, plant, go the other way — make them believe the fake." Success metric: create 2+ steps of separation on 4/6 dodges.
Focal 1v1 Dodging
42–55
3v3 Small-Sided Scrimmage (Constraint: Must Scoop Before Shooting)
Half-field 3v3. Every possession starts with a ground ball at midfield. Constraint: the team that scoops the ball must complete one pass before they can shoot. No face-offs — coach rolls a new ball after every goal or turnover. This forces players to scoop under pressure, transition quickly, and look for an outlet. Coaching cue: "Scoop, turn, find the outlet in one motion."
Scrimmage
55–60
Cool-Down & Message
Players circle up. Coach picks one player who scooped cleanly under pressure and calls them out by name. Teach point: "Ground balls win games. If you scoop with your feet moving, you're already ahead." Hand out water, remind them of the next practice date.
Cool-Down

COACHING NOTES
This Week's Priorities
  • Teach ground-ball technique BEFORE the GB battle — players need to know the mechanics before the pressure arrives.
  • Box position on every catch: stick vertical, top hand at the armpit. This is the one receiving position to install in Week 1.
  • One reliable dodge: fake-and-go. Don't introduce roll dodges, split dodges, or face dodges until the fake-and-go is clean.
  • Goalie builds confidence with soft feeds from 8–10 yards. No game-speed shooting until they catch 8/10 clean.
  • Constraint on the scrimmage (pass before shooting) keeps the chaos structured. Remove the constraint only if players are completing passes consistently.
Evaluation Notes
  • Watch for players bending at the waist instead of dropping their hips on ground balls — this is the #1 scooping error at U12.
  • Track which players naturally find the outlet after a GB vs. which ones hold the ball and stall — the outlet-finders are your transition players.
  • In the 1v1 dodging drill, note which players sell the fake (defender bites) vs. which ones telegraph the dodge (defender doesn't move).
  • During the scrimmage, log which players scoop cleanly under pressure vs. which ones bobble or stop their feet — this is your GB-starter list for Week 2.
  • Goalie: count saves vs. shots faced. If save rate is under 30%, the goalie is guessing instead of tracking the ball.
  • Self-eval: Did you give one clear cue during the scrimmage, or did you over-coach? Write your one cue for Week 2 before you leave the field.
Watch For (U12-Specific Failure Modes)
  • Players bend at the waist instead of dropping the hips on GBs — cue "butt down" every rep until it's automatic.
  • Ball-carrier carries with the top hand on the plastic (choked up) — cue "hand down the shaft" every time you see it.
  • Players lock onto one target and telegraph the pass — teach "catch, look at the goal, then pass" to break the tunnel vision.
  • Goalie backs up into the crease when a shot is coming — this shrinks their save radius. Cue "step to the ball, not away from it."
  • In the scrimmage, players hold the ball after a scoop instead of turning and finding the outlet — this kills transition. Cue "scoop, turn, outlet" as one motion.
  • Coach freezing when the scrimmage gets chaotic — write down your one coaching cue before practice and commit to saying only that during live play.
