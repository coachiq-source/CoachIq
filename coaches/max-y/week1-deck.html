Week 1 Field Sheet
Week 1 Focus: Ground balls, box-position catching, and one reliable dodge under pressure
Outcome: Players scoop ground balls with their feet moving and their butt down, catch passes in box position without bobbling, and know one reliable dodge they can run when their first look is taken away.
First Game
May 2
Primary Target
First winning season
Field
Not specified
Conditioning
U12 attention cap is ~7 min per block. Conditioning happens inside the skill work — ground-ball battles and 1v1 drills are the cardio.
Goalie Track
Developing goalie — soft feeds from 8–10 yards, focusing on catching mechanics. Joins team for all live scrimmage reps.
Week 1 Practice (60 min) Fundamentals
0–8: Arrival & Dynamic Warm-Up — high knees, butt kicks, side shuffle, carioca, partner passing
8–18: Noodle Scooping — butt down, top hand down, scoop through it · 8 reps per player
18–28: Partner Passing (Box Position) — catch two hands, stick up, look it in · 20 completions per pair
28–42: 1v1 Dodging (Fake & Go) — fake hard, plant, go the other way · 6 reps per player
42–55: 3v3 Scrimmage (Constraint: Must Scoop Before Shooting) — scoop, turn, find the outlet in one motion
55–60: Cool-Down & Message — call out one player who scooped cleanly under pressure
Drill Key Cue What This Teaches
Noodle Scooping "Butt down, top hand down, scoop through it." Ground balls win games — clean scoops with feet moving
Partner Passing "Catch two hands, stick up, look it in." Box position keeps the stick protected and ready
1v1 Dodging "Fake hard, plant, go the other way." One reliable dodge when pressure arrives
3v3 Scrimmage "Scoop, turn, find the outlet in one motion." Small-sided games with constraints = structured chaos
Tracking
GB Scoop Rate: Clean scoops / total attempts
Box Catches: Stick vertical, top hand at armpit
1v1 Separation: 2+ steps on 4/6 dodges
Decisions
Identify your top 3 ground-ball players by name — they start every GB battle in Week 2.
Watch For
Players bending at the waist instead of dropping hips on GBs
Ball-carrier choked up on the plastic — cue "hand down the shaft"
Players holding the ball after scoop instead of turning and finding outlet
